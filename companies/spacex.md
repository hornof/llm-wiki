## What It Is

Space-launch + satellite-internet company founded by [[elon-musk|Elon Musk]] in 2002. **Wiki-tracked primarily as the operator of substrate-tier AI compute capacity** following its 2025 acquisition of [[xai|xAI]] and the subsequent emergence of **Colossus 1** (Memphis-area data center, ~300MW) as the operational substrate for frontier-AI training + inference at multi-hyperscaler scale.

## Recent Activity

- **2025: Acquired [[xai|xAI]]**, removing xAI from independent-AI-company indices like the Forbes AI 50 — [[forbes-ai-50-2026]]. **First wiki-captured vertical-integration event** combining a frontier-AI lab with a substrate-tier compute provider.
- **2026-05-07: Anthropic-xAI Colossus deal — entire Colossus 1 capacity contracted to [[anthropic|Anthropic]]** for frontier-model training. ~$5B/yr per Daily Brief synopses (corroborated by Latent Space AINews); Willison primary omits pricing. *Notable*: Musk publicly states he reserves the right to *"reclaim the compute if their AI engages in actions that harm humanity"* — non-commercial discretionary clause inside an otherwise commercial deal. — [[willison-anthropic-xai-colossus-2026-05]]
- **2026-06-04: Goldman Sachs forecasts SpaceX AI revenue $322B by 2030** driven by compute-as-a-service offerings. **First wiki-captured concrete dollar-forecast for SpaceX AI revenue trajectory.** Skepticism noted on Goldman's IPO underwriting role potentially biasing the forecast upward. — [[dailybrief-roundup-2026-06-04]]
- **2026-06-05: Google commits $920M/month (~$11B/yr) to SpaceX for compute** ([[google-spacex-920m-monthly-compute-2026-06-05]]). **First wiki-captured concrete-dollar Google→SpaceX compute deal**. *"Google's own datacenters hit a wall"* — first wiki-captured frontier-hyperscaler admission that owned-infrastructure cannot keep up with AI demand. **Operational confirmation of the substrate-tier compute lock-in thesis** ([[anthropic-spacex-higher-limits-2026-05-06]]).
- **2026-06-06: Musk posts satirical 3-step AI-profitability plan** centered on buying massive GPUs — *"industry observers noted the joke mirrors xAI's real-world scaling"* ([[dailybrief-roundup-2026-06-06]]). First wiki-captured Musk self-satire on xAI scaling.

## Why The Wiki Tracks SpaceX

SpaceX is the **substrate-tier compute monopoly-candidate** for the frontier-AI era. As of mid-2026:

- **3-customer Colossus-tier consolidation event articulated**: xAI internal + Anthropic ($5B/yr) + Google ($11B/yr) = ~$16B+/yr revenue base before counting xAI internal-use
- **Goldman's $322B by 2030 forecast** implies additional capacity-scaling
- **First wiki-captured concrete vertical-integration event** (SpaceX-xAI merger) combined with **first wiki-captured concrete hyperscaler off-balance-sheet compute deals** (Anthropic + Google)

Pattern-watch: Microsoft / AWS / Meta / Apple as candidate next-customers; whether the *"reclaim the compute"* clause from the Anthropic deal generalizes to other contracts.

## Key Products / Infrastructure

- **Colossus 1** — Memphis-area data center; reported 300MW capacity. As of mid-2026, contracted to Anthropic (training) + Google (compute-as-a-service)
- **xAI** — Wholly-owned subsidiary; Grok model family + Grok Imagine video-agent product. See [[xai]] for the AI-lab-side coverage
- **Starlink** — satellite-internet (not directly wiki-tracked but adjacent to bandwidth-side context for distributed-AI workloads)

## Cross-cutting framings

- **Substrate-tier compute lock-in framing operationally validated**: Anthropic + Google both go off-balance-sheet for SpaceX-tier capacity. First wiki-captured concrete demonstration of the [[anthropic-spacex-higher-limits-2026-05-06|substrate-tier lock-in thesis]] at the largest-hyperscaler scale.
- **Vertical-integration thesis confirmation**: SpaceX-xAI = vertically-integrated substrate (Colossus) + agent layer (Grok / Imagine). Other vendors compete *upstream* (foundation models) or *downstream* (applications) but lack SpaceX's substrate-tier vertical integration.
- **Geographic concentration risk**: Memphis-area substrate concentration pairs with [[dailybrief-roundup-2026-06-05|teortaxesTex's compact-cluster-vs-strategically-vulnerable-datacenter framing]]. Pattern-watch: does SpaceX diversify geography, or does single-site concentration become the substrate-tier weak point?
- **Reputational drag on Colossus 1**: ongoing political controversy — gas turbines reportedly operated without Clean Air Act permits; reporting links the facility to increased hospital admissions in surrounding Memphis communities (Andy Masley quote via Willison: *"I would simply not run my computing out of this specific data center."*).
