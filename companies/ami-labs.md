## What It Is
AI research lab founded by Yann LeCun in November 2025 after his departure from Meta. Focused on world-model architectures and human-like artificial intelligence as an alternative to the LLM-centric paradigm. Executive Chair: Yann LeCun. CEO: Alex LeBrun. **Motto: "AI for the real world"** ([[lecun-after-llms-unsupervised-learning-2026-05-15]]). **Headquartered in Paris**; American office in New York (explicitly not Silicon Valley) — LeCun's stated reason: *"escape the herd behavior where everybody is digging the same trench."*

- Co-led by Cathay Innovation, Greycroft, Hiro Capital, HV Capital, and Bezos Expeditions

## Research Direction
World-model architectures — specifically Joint Embedding Predictive Architecture (JEPA). LeCun's thesis: LLMs are "a dead end when it comes to superintelligence"; world models that build internal representations of how reality works are the right path.

## Roadmap (May 2026)

Per LeCun's [[lecun-after-llms-unsupervised-learning-2026-05-15|Unsupervised Learning interview May 2026]]:

- **Within ~1 year**: a general methodology to train **hierarchical world models** on a wide variety of modalities.
- **12–18 months**: scaled demonstrations of **action-conditioned world models** for use cases in robotics, industrial process control, and healthcare — with industry partners (some already investors).
- **5-year framing**: *"complete world domination, essentially"* — explicit Linus Torvalds Linux-analogy joke. The serious claim underneath: world-model-based systems will be the dominant architecture for "AI for the real world" by ~2031.
- **Latest research surface**: SIGREG (Sketched Isotropic Gaussian Regularization) for preventing JEPA representation collapse; LEWorldModel paper as recommended primary read (Randall Balestriero + MILA student).

## Sister Project: Tapestry

Separate from AMI Labs but adjacent: **Tapestry** is LeCun's federated-learning open-platform LLM thesis where contributors share parameter vectors (not data) toward a global consensus model. Pitched as sovereign-AI infrastructure for countries that want neither US nor China model dependence. **No public release timeline.** First wiki capture: [[lecun-after-llms-unsupervised-learning-2026-05-15]]. AMI is about world models for the physical world; Tapestry is about federated training for the language/knowledge layer. Both are LeCun-led but architecturally and organizationally distinct.
