## What It Is

**Thinking Machines Lab** is a frontier AI lab founded by **[[mira-murati|Mira Murati]]** (former OpenAI CTO). Its stated mission — *"build AI that extends human will and judgment"* — leans toward **customization and human-in-the-loop** framing rather than a race to the single strongest model. It first surfaced on the wiki via its **"The Future Worth Building Is Human"** manifesto ([[dailybrief-roundup-2026-07-11]], deferred as a create-candidate); the **2026-07-15 launch of the [[inkling|Inkling]] open-weights model** crossed the threshold to a tracked entity.

## Traction Signals

- **2026-07-15 — first product: [[inkling|Inkling]]**, an **open-weights** 975B-MoE (41B active) model + a **Tinker** fine-tuning platform, released with full weights on Hugging Face and broad inference-partner support ([[thinking-machines-inkling-open-weights-launch-2026-07-16]]). Choosing to ship a *deliberately-not-#1, customization-first open* model is itself the positioning signal — *"a good open-weights base for customization."*
- **Frontier-lab open-weights release** read by the [[dailybrief-roundup-2026-07-16|brief]] as a *"credibility move, not a commodity one."*
- **Tinker** fine-tuning platform (50%-off launch promo) — the lab's commercial wedge is *adaptation infrastructure*, not just a model.
