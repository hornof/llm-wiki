## Who They Are
Chinese-born American computer scientist and professor at Stanford. Best known for creating ImageNet — the large-scale visual dataset that catalyzed the deep learning revolution in the 2010s. Co-director of Stanford HAI (Human-Centered AI Institute). Currently focused on World Labs, a startup developing "spatial intelligence" AI (3D physical world reasoning). Named Time 100 AI Most Influential People (2023) and one of Time's "Architects of AI" (2025).

## Their Current Focus
World Labs (on partial leave from Stanford since Jan 2024): developing spatial intelligence AI — systems that can reason about and act within three-dimensional environments. Raised $230M (2024) and $1B (2026). Also advocating for science-based AI governance rather than "science-fiction" framing.

## Notable Takes
- **ImageNet**: "The lack of large, annotated datasets was the key bottleneck in computer vision" — built 14M labeled images to solve it — [[wikipedia-fei-fei-li]]
- **Human-centered AI**: "I believe in human-centered AI to benefit people in positive and benevolent ways. It is deeply against my principles to work on any project that I think is to weaponize AI." — [[wikipedia-fei-fei-li]]
- **AI governance (Feb 2025)**: "AI governance should be based on science rather than science fiction" — [[wikipedia-fei-fei-li]]
- **On diversity in AI**: "We are at a turning point. AI's influence continues to grow, but representation and inclusion does not." — [[wikipedia-fei-fei-li]]

## Key Contributions
- **ImageNet** (2007–): 14M+ labeled images, 22K categories; enabled ILSVRC competition that launched modern deep learning era
- **Stanford HAI**: Human-Centered AI Institute — research, education, and policy for beneficial AI
- **AI4ALL** (2017): nonprofit for diversity and inclusion in AI education; co-founded with Melinda French Gates and Jensen Huang
- **World Labs** (2024): spatial intelligence AI startup; $1B raised by 2026
- **CS231n**: Stanford's Deep Learning for Computer Vision course — widely available online
