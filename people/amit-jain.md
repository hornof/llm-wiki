## Who They Are
Co-founder and CEO of Luma AI. Background in computer vision and machine learning. Building what he calls multimodal general intelligence — AI that can generate, understand, and operate in the physical world by jointly modeling language, audio, video, and images. Luma raised $900M Series C in November 2025 at ~$4B valuation.

## Their Current Focus
Building the "Unified Intelligence" model family (Uni-1) — a single jointly-trained model across all modalities. Rejects the industry pattern of language-first models retrofitted for other modalities. Framing: world models are a necessary condition (but not sufficient) for AGI.

## Notable Takes
- **Against language-backbone retrofitting**: "Instead of language backbones retrofitting everything, we need a unified, singular latent space where we can think and reason about all different pieces of information as one." — [[cognitive-revolution-luma-worldsim]]
- **On joint training**: "Unless you're able to jointly train and backprop through, it's very difficult to imagine how the sum of the parts is better than the parts themselves." — [[amplify-amit-jain-interview]]
- **On AGI**: "A world model is a necessary condition, but not a sufficient condition for AGI."
- **Intelligence in pixels**: Uni-1 should "think in language and imagine and render in pixels" — unifying reasoning and generation in one model — [[techcrunch-luma-uni1]]
- **On interpretability**: Post-hoc interpretability is "archaeology"; productive interpretability means understanding training dynamics and dataset engineering — [[cognitive-revolution-luma-worldsim]]
- **On model size vs. data**: Rather than scaling models 100-1000x larger, train on vastly more data — efficiency over brute-force scaling — [[amplify-amit-jain-interview]]
