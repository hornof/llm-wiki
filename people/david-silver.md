## Who They Are

AI researcher; former Google DeepMind. Spent 13 years at DeepMind and was the principal architect of three landmark reinforcement learning systems: AlphaGo (defeated world Go champion Lee Sedol; "Move 37" — a move with 1-in-10,000 probability of being played by any human), AlphaZero (learned chess from scratch using only the rules; beat the world's strongest chess engine within hours), and AlphaStar (reached grandmaster level in StarCraft II against professionals). Left DeepMind in 2026 to found [[ineffable-intelligence]].

## Their Current Focus

Building [[ineffable-intelligence]] — a "superlearner" AI that discovers knowledge entirely from self-play and experience, without pre-training on human-generated data. The founding thesis is that the entire current AI industry is building toward a ceiling by training on human data, and that genuinely superhuman intelligence requires the same approach that produced Move 37: learning from first principles with no human-generated training signal.

## Notable Takes

- **LLMs have a hard ceiling**: ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and Grok can "remix human knowledge" but "CANNOT discover something genuinely new" because their training signal is human-generated text — [[sources/ric-rtp-david-silver-ineffable]]
- **RL from self-play is the path**: the approach behind AlphaGo/AlphaZero/AlphaStar — learning only from rules and self-play — is the only demonstrated path to discovering knowledge beyond the human frontier — [[sources/ric-rtp-david-silver-ineffable]]
- **Mission framing**: "To make first contact with superintelligence" — [[sources/ric-rtp-david-silver-ineffable]]
