## Who They Are

YC Group Partner. Background: previously growth at Airbnb. Authored the **"AI-Native Service Companies"** entry in [[yc-summer-2026-rfs]] — the category multiple practitioners (including commenters on [[benln-yc-summer-2026-rfs]]) flagged as the standout underrated opportunity in the entire 2026 RFS.

## Their Current Focus

Funding founders building companies that **don't sell software — they sell the service.** "AI-native service companies" as the next category shift after the 2023–2025 era of AI copilots. Target verticals: insurance brokerage, accounting/tax/audit, compliance, healthcare administration.

## Notable Takes

- **AI-Native Service Companies RFS** ([[yc-summer-2026-rfs]], April 2026): **"AI models are improving really fast, and they're now able to do complex work far beyond engineering. Historically, services became SaaS software. More recently, they became AI copilots… What we're excited about now is the next step: AI-native companies that don't sell software — they sell the service."** Two structural reasons it's the right next move: (1) total spend on services >> spend on software, (2) most services are already outsourced, making them structurally easier to replace with an AI-native product.
- Direct contrast with the [[ai-native-organizations]] / [[block]] thesis (which is about *how an AI-native company runs internally*) — Gustaf's framing is *what an AI-native company sells*. Both relevant; orthogonal axes.
- Aligns with [[saas-disruption-thesis]] (Naval, April 2026): if AI collapses the cost of producing software by 10–100x, the durable value migrates to delivering outcomes, not selling tools.
