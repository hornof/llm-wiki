## Who They Are

Product Manager at DoorDash; former Google APM. Author of the **In the Weeds** Substack (`hannahstulberg.substack.com`), including the viral *"Claude Code for Everything"* series. Runs a Maven workshop on **how to build a Team OS in Claude Code**. First wiki capture: [[stahlberg-team-os-aakash-2026-05]] (Aakash Gupta podcast + followup essay, May 2026). Documents **~1,500 hours of personal Claude Code usage** — the highest single-practitioner usage captured in the wiki.

**Spelling note**: appears as both *Stulberg* (her Substack URL, Maven profile) and *Stahlberg* (the @aakashgupta podcast transcript). **Canonical: Stulberg.**

## Their Current Focus

Team OS architecture in Claude Code — shared, function-organized repos where every team function checks in canonical context and anyone on the team can query it in natural language. *"Making myself unnecessary for context questions."* Cross-functional collapse PM: one PM now supports 10+ engineers and interfaces with sales, marketing, support; the Team OS is the artifact that scales context across the boundary.

## Notable Takes

- **"Claude Code is the most misleading name in AI"** (May 2026): because it's not just for code. The Team OS pattern is the load-bearing existence-proof of that framing — Claude Code is used for PM workflows, design context, support summaries, customer-call transcripts. — [[stahlberg-team-os-aakash-2026-05]]
- **"The biggest mistake [PMs make with Claude Code] is that people give up too early"** (May 2026). Practitioner observation from 1,500 hours of usage. — [[stahlberg-team-os-aakash-2026-05]]
- **CLAUDE.md should be very, very, very lean** (May 2026): root CLAUDE.md contains a doc-index, team roster + Slack handles, and key Slack-channel purposes. Everything else loads progressively from function-folder sub-CLAUDE.mds as natural-language queries reference them. Independent practitioner validation of [[claude-md-pattern|the minimalist CLAUDE.md discipline]] from the team-repo angle. — [[stahlberg-team-os-aakash-2026-05]]
- **"Make yourself unnecessary, not indispensable"** (May 2026): inverts standard PM advice. Stulberg specifically frames being asleep/offline and having a new engineer answer their 3-month-old customer-decision question in 15 seconds without paging her as the **target state**, not a degradation. — [[stahlberg-team-os-aakash-2026-05]]
- **Context management as the binding skill** (May 2026): names four context primitives — what's in the session, what gets loaded next, what stays out, how context degrades over a long session. Teaches **"context 101"** as the foundational skill for Claude-Code-for-PM-work. — [[stahlberg-team-os-aakash-2026-05]]
