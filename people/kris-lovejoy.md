## Who They Are

Kris Lovejoy is Global Strategy Leader at [[kyndryl]] — the IT-infrastructure services company spun out from IBM in 2021. Her career has been at the intersection of IT infrastructure and security; she is one of the people enterprises call when they want to move from AI experimentation to real deployment. First wiki capture via her 2026-05-15 *Eye on A.I.* interview with [[craig-smith|Craig Smith]] — [[eye-on-ai-lovejoy-agentic-enterprise-2026-05-15]].

## Their Current Focus

- **Enterprise agentic-AI deployment readiness** — the practical gap between successful pilots and production-scale systems.
- **IT Service Management as the highest-ROI entry point** for agentic adoption in regulated enterprises.
- **Security risks reframed as configuration / context / human-error** rather than sophisticated external attack.

## Notable Takes

- **"Agentic AI is a bullet train sitting on tracks built for 30 miles per hour"** (May 15 2026): the cleanest articulation captured to date of the enterprise-readiness gap. The technology is ready; most organizations aren't; the pipe-to-production gap is wider than the hype suggests. — [[eye-on-ai-lovejoy-agentic-enterprise-2026-05-15]]
- **ITSM is the smartest agentic entry point**: workflows are well-defined, failure modes are bounded and observable, **up to 90% cost savings** in IT service management produce the budget that funds broader modernization. The implied ROI loop: agents → ITSM cost reduction → modernization budget → broader deployment.
- **Security risk is configuration, not capability**: practical agentic security failures are dominated by misconfiguration, bad context, and human error — not sophisticated external attackers. Calibration anchor against the [[google-criminal-hackers-ai-vuln-2026-05-11|AI-vulnerability-discovery]] thread (real, but rare compared to operator-error failure modes in deployed systems).
- **Half of traditional IT administration tasks handled by AI agents by 2031**: dated, falsifiable prediction. Slots into the wiki's timeline-calibration triangle alongside [[andrej-karpathy|Karpathy's 10-year-to-replacement]] framing ([[zephyr-karpathy-10-year-agent-window-2026-05-14]]) and [[import-ai-456-clark-rsi-radical-optionality-2026-05-11|Korinek's ~6-year explosive regime]] scenario.
- **"Not coders, but people trained to ask the right questions"** will thrive in the agentic era — direct counter-prediction to the [[anthropic-labor-market-impacts-2026-03-05|Computer Programmers 75% covered]] finding from Anthropic's `observed exposure` measure. The displacement maps to current programmer roles; the replacement profile requires different skills entirely.
