## Their Current Focus

- Practitioner-facing documentation of [[loop-engineering]] primitives — turning the sprawling third-party "designing loops" discourse into an Anthropic-canonical framework tied to concrete Claude Code commands (`/goal`, `/loop`, `/schedule`, dynamic workflows, auto mode).

## Notable Takes

- **Official Anthropic loop definition (Jul 6 2026)**: *"loops as agents repeating cycles of work until a stop condition is met"* — categorized by trigger, stop criteria, primitive used, and task fit. See [[loop-engineering]].
- **Four loop types with a "hand-off" framing**: turn-based (hand off *the check*) → goal-based (*the stop condition*) → time-based (*the trigger*) → proactive (*the prompt*). The framing reframes loop design as *deciding which piece of your own judgment you're comfortable delegating*.
- **System over prompt**: *"The quality of a loop's output depends on the system around it"* — clean codebase, self-verification skills, reachable docs, and a fresh-context second agent for review. When a result misses the bar, encode the fix into the system, not just the one issue.
