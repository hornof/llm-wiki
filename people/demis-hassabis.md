## Who They Are
CEO of Google DeepMind. Nobel laureate. PhD in neuroscience. Co-founded DeepMind (acquired by Google), which produced AlphaGo, AlphaFold, and systems that won gold medals at the International Math Olympiad. Takes a neuroscience-grounded approach to AI — studies the human brain as the only confirmed existence proof of general intelligence.

## Their Current Focus
Building toward AGI at Google DeepMind. Publicly holds a stricter definition of AGI than most in the industry, which he maintains has not changed. Focused on the fundamental gaps between current systems and true general intelligence: continual learning, creativity, long-term planning.

## Notable Takes
- **AGI definition**: "A system that can exhibit all the cognitive capabilities humans can" — explicitly stricter than most lab CEOs — [[thread-milkroadai-hassabis-agi]]
- **Jagged intelligence**: current AI systems "spike brilliantly in one area and collapse in another based on how a question is posed" — not general intelligence — [[thread-milkroadai-hassabis-agi]]
- **The Einstein Test**: the real measure of AGI is whether a system trained on all knowledge up to 1911 could independently discover general relativity by 1915 — current models cannot — [[thread-milkroadai-hassabis-agi]]
- **AGI timeline**: 5–10 years, contingent on 1–2 fundamental breakthroughs beyond scaling (continual learning, efficient memory, long-term reasoning) — [[thread-milkroadai-hassabis-agi]]
- **Scale of impact**: "10x the Industrial Revolution, at 10x the speed" — [[thread-milkroadai-hassabis-agi]]
