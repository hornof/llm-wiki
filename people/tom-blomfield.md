## Who They Are

YC Group Partner. Authored the **"Company Brain"** entry in [[yc-summer-2026-rfs]] — the primary source for the phrase that has since propagated as a YC-named startup category. Prior career: founder of Monzo (UK challenger bank) and GoCardless; brought across the Atlantic into YC partnership.

## Their Current Focus

Funding founders building the missing layer between fragmented company data and reliable AI automation. Specifically: a system that pulls knowledge out of all fragmented sources, structures it, keeps it current, and **turns it into an executable skills file for AI**. Living maps of how companies work — not company search, not chatbot-over-docs.

## Notable Takes

- **Company Brain RFS** ([[yc-summer-2026-rfs]], April 2026): **"The biggest blocker to AI automation of companies is no longer the models, they just got so good so quickly. Now the blocker is the domain knowledge."** Frames the gap as the missing primitive between scattered data and executable agents. Directly cited or paralleled by [[ashwingop]] in the [[company-brain]] longform writing. — [[yc-summer-2026-rfs]]
- **"Garry's G-Brain, but for every business in the world"**: invokes Garry Tan's earlier internal framing about a personal/company brain primitive. Implies YC has been workshopping this concept internally before publishing as RFS.
- **"Skills file for AI"** as the unit of company-brain output: each company gets a structured, current, executable skills definition that AI agents read and operate against. Aligns with [[claude-code]]/[[claude-desktop]] CLAUDE.md schema-driven configuration as the natural format.
