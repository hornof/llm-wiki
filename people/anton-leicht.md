## Who They Are

Anton Leicht is an independent AI-policy analyst writing *Threading the Needle* on Substack (`writing.antonleicht.me`). Beat: AI governance, geopolitics of compute, frontier-AI access regimes. First wiki-tracked external-analyst voice on the policy side — complements [[jack-clark]]'s lab-internal Import AI thread by sitting *outside* the labs and naming the access-restriction patterns from a structural-analyst lens.

## Their Current Focus

- **Frontier-AI access constraints** — the *Cut Off* essay ([[antonleicht-frontier-ai-access-cut-off-2026-05-13]]) names three compounding gates: security-driven restrictions, economic compute scarcity, US-government strategic gatekeeping.
- **Distillation-driven sustained restriction** — security drives *initial* access restriction, but the distillation-risk model (DeepSeek named as the canonical case) sustains it indefinitely *regardless* of cost trajectory.
- **Geopolitics of compute** — non-US-ally compute-for-access trades; middle-power sovereign-AI contingency; explicit-disadvantage analysis for European AI deployment.
- **Prior writing references**: "import imperatives" and geopolitical AI competition framings.

## Notable Takes

- **Three compounding gates on frontier-AI access (May 2026)**: security restrictions + compute economics + US strategic gatekeeping converge to *structural* gatekeeping that rivals industrial-revolution-scale haves/have-nots inequality. Counterweight to the "AI will commoditize" thesis at the frontier tier. — [[antonleicht-frontier-ai-access-cut-off-2026-05-13]]
- **Distillation-risk keeps the gate up indefinitely**: even if costs fall, the *risk model* (competitors extracting capabilities via API access) keeps frontier-tier access selectively restricted. Names a **6-9 month distillation lag**; models become cheaper "next year" but frontier capabilities grow *"more expensive month-to-month for years now."* — [[antonleicht-frontier-ai-access-cut-off-2026-05-13]]
- **Named mechanisms**: Anthropic Mythos (selective US-firm cybersecurity access), OpenAI Daybreak (analogous), GAIN Act (US right-of-first-refusal for American chips), NSA predeployment review, KYC restrictions on frontier-model access. The OpenAI Daybreak naming is the first wiki-captured parallel to [[claude-mythos]]'s selective-deployment pattern.
- **Compute-for-frontier-access trades**: non-US allies should negotiate subsidized-energy / compute-infrastructure deals for guaranteed frontier-tier access; middle powers should maintain sovereign-AI contingency capabilities.
- **Headline quote**: *"AI tokens will be strategically and economically central to all future societies, so we should do our best to enable their free flow."*
