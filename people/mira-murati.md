## Who They Are

Mira Murati is the founder of **[[thinking-machines|Thinking Machines Lab]]** and the former **CTO of OpenAI**, where she led product and research engineering through the GPT-3.5/4 and ChatGPT era. Her lab is positioned around **customization and human agency** rather than a pure race for the strongest frontier model.

## Their Current Focus

- **Thinking Machines Lab** — mission *"build AI that extends human will and judgment."* First manifesto: *"The Future Worth Building Is Human"* ([[dailybrief-roundup-2026-07-11]]).
- **2026-07-15 — [[inkling|Inkling]] open-weights launch** ([[thinking-machines-inkling-open-weights-launch-2026-07-16]]): the lab ships a 975B-MoE open-weights model + the **Tinker** fine-tuning platform — an open, customization-first bet rather than closed frontier supremacy. *(Murati is not named in the Inkling announcement itself; the lab is publicly hers.)*

## Notable Takes

- **Human-agency framing**: the lab's throughline — AI that *extends* human will and judgment, and *"keeping humans in the loop when the AI is faster and cheaper"* (per the 07-11 manifesto coverage). Rhymes with [[andrew-ng|Ng]]'s "context advantage" and the [[loop-engineering|"stay the engineer"]] caution.
- **Open-weights as strategy**: shipping a deliberately-not-#1 open base — *"so that people can make it their own"* — positions adaptation infrastructure (Tinker) as the moat over raw model rank.
