## Who They Are

Programmer and blogger writing at jefftk.com. EA-adjacent practitioner voice; first-principles essayist focused on infrastructure, norms, and operational dynamics where AI changes underlying assumptions. First wiki appearance via the May 2026 vulnerability-cultures essay.

## Their Current Focus

Vulnerability disclosure and security norms under AI-driven analysis. May 2026 essay names the structural collision between AI-assisted commit-stream analysis and the disclosure norms that previously protected the patch window.

## Notable Takes

- **AI is breaking two vulnerability cultures** (May 2026): both **Coordinated Disclosure** (90-day embargo) and **"Bugs are Bugs"** (Linux's quick-fix-without-attention norm) relied on assumptions about how long it takes humans to notice fixes or rediscover vulnerabilities. AI collapses both timelines. Concrete example: the Copy Fail vulnerability had a *second independent report nine hours after disclosure*. Path forward: very short embargoes that get progressively shorter — *"AI acceleration cuts both ways: it accelerates attackers' rediscovery, but it also lets defenders ship and verify patches faster."* — [[jefftk-vulnerability-cultures-2026-05-08]]
