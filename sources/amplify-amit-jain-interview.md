## Summary
Amplify Partners interview with Amit Jain on Luma AI's philosophy. Covers joint training rationale, multimodal pre-training approach, visual data scale, and Jain's view that efficiency and data volume matter more than model size.

## Key Claims / Takeaways
- **Joint training imperative**: "Unless you're able to jointly train and backprop through, it's very difficult to imagine how the sum of the parts is better than the parts themselves"
- **Multimodal pre-training**: audio, video, language, and digital content trained collectively from scratch; yields "way better reasoning capabilities" than LLMs alone
- **Visual data scale**: Dream Machine V2 trained on 1.2 quintillion tokens across petabytes of data
- **Efficiency over size**: train intelligent models on vastly more data rather than scaling model size 100-1000x; enables faster, cheaper deployment
- **Kardashev framing**: Jain frames advanced AI as essential infrastructure for manipulating larger-scale physical systems
