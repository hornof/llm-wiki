## Summary

[[naveen-rao]] amplifies [[peter-mcmahon]]'s [[mcmahon-1000x-energy-efficiency-2026-05|1000x energy-efficiency essay]] from [[unconventional-ai]]. Confirms the core stance: "we're building in the open" and "it's the interaction of the model and the physics of the hardware. We need to do it all to realize 1000x." Frames the difficulty as the point — "summiting K2 is only worth doing because it's hard."

## Key Claims / Takeaways

- **Frames Unconventional AI's mission as full-stack co-design**: "We have to go up and down the stack… it's not one magic bullet."
- **"Building in the open"** — public posture for an early-stage hardware company, contra typical hardware-startup secrecy.
- **Two hard constraints stated as the design problem** (also in McMahon's essay):
  1. Data movement dominates energy
  2. Amdahl's Law caps system-level gains
- **Comments surface adjacent thinking**:
  - **Boy Kester**: "the coordination layer is where Amdahl's law actually bites" — coordination physics, not raw compute, is the bottleneck
  - **Sidney Scott** (early Rain AI backer): distinguishes the "substrate face" of the mountain (chip-level co-design — where Unconventional AI is) from the "horizontal traverse" (fleet-level orchestration across heterogeneous hardware) — same governing physics, different ascent
  - **Duane Schwingel**: "Once data movement costs dominate arithmetic costs, optimization stops being about isolated components and starts becoming about orchestration" — convergent framing with Sentra's Company Brain pace argument from a different scale
