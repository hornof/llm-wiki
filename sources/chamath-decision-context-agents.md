## Summary

Chamath Palihapitiya argues that the missing layer in AI-assisted software development is not code-writing speed but the capture of decision reasoning — the "why" behind architectural choices. This reasoning typically lives in Slack threads, Linear tickets, or individual heads, preventing team knowledge from compounding. He promotes 8090's "Software Factory" (factory.8090.ai) as a multiplayer, AI-enabled solution to this problem.

## Key Claims / Takeaways

- The bottleneck in AI-assisted software development is not execution speed but decision-context capture
- Architectural reasoning ("why we made this choice") is typically lost in Slack threads, Linear tickets, or individual memory
- Without captured reasoning, individual velocity increases but team collective knowledge does not compound — the team goes the same speed or slower
- A good multiplayer AI dev experience captures the "why" layer, not just executes the "what" faster
- 8090's Software Factory (factory.8090.ai) is positioned as this multiplayer, AI-enabled decision-context layer
- Comment thread consensus: this gap exists everywhere in enterprise knowledge work, not only in software
