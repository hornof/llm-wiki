## Summary

Latent Space episode with **Andy Beam & Rafa Gómez-Bombarelli of Lila Sciences** arguing that **science — not the internet — is the next training-data frontier**: automate the *physical* experiment loop (wet-lab robotics + ML) so labs generate high-signal ground-truth data at scale, *"operating like data centers."* Anchor source for the new [[science-as-training-data-frontier]] concept. **Surfaced 3×** (07-16 / 07-17 / 07-20 briefs) — elevated on the 3rd recurrence per the standing create-candidate note.

## Key Claims / Takeaways

- **The bet**: lab robots become **training-data factories**, not just faster analysis pipelines. *"The real insight isn't that labs should feel like data centers — it's that they need to operate like them."*
- **Where the bottleneck moves**: in biotech/materials, the constraint *"isn't compute or models; it's the cost of generating ground truth in the wet world."* Automating the *experiment* loop (generating the phenomena) — not just the analysis — *"changes what problems are even worth solving."*
- **New moat**: *"the constraint moves from 'do we have enough training data' to 'can we instrument + standardize collection fast enough.'"* Data-generation capacity, not model access, becomes the differentiator.

## Why it matters

- **A data-frontier answer to internet-text exhaustion**: as web text saturates, physically-generated scientific data is a fresh, high-signal, defensible source — see [[science-as-training-data-frontier]].
