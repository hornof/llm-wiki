## Summary
Wikipedia article on Yann LeCun — co-inventor of convolutional neural networks (CNNs/LeNet), Turing Award winner (2018), former Chief AI Scientist at Meta, now founder of AMI Labs (Advanced Machine Intelligence Labs, $1.03B raised March 2026). One of the "Godfathers of Deep Learning" alongside Bengio, Hinton, and Schmidhuber. Vocal critic of LLMs as a path to superintelligence; advocates world-model architectures instead.

## Key Claims / Takeaways
- Invented convolutional neural networks (LeNet) at Bell Labs; deployed commercially for bank check recognition
- Turing Award 2018 (shared with Bengio and Hinton) for making deep neural networks a critical component of computing
- Chief AI Scientist at Meta/FAIR for 12 years (2013–2025)
- Left Meta November 2025 to found AMI Labs (Advanced Machine Intelligence Labs) — focused on world-model architectures
- AMI Labs raised $1.03B at $3.5B pre-money valuation (March 2026), co-led by Bezos Expeditions and others
- Notable quote (FT interview): "I'm sure there's a lot of people at Meta who would like me to NOT tell the world that LLMs basically are a dead end when it comes to superintelligence."
- One of four "Godfathers of AI/Deep Learning" — alongside Bengio, Hinton, Schmidhuber
- Co-founded ICLR (2012) with Yoshua Bengio
- VinFuture Prize 2024 shared with Bengio, Jensen Huang, Hinton, and Fei-Fei Li
- Princess of Asturias Award 2022 shared with Bengio, Hinton, and Demis Hassabis
