## Summary
Sequoia Capital partner Konstantine Buhler interviews Demis Hassabis, co-founder and CEO of Google DeepMind, at Sequoia's "AI Ascent" event. Hassabis traces his origins — chess prodigy, game developer at Bullfrog/Elixir Studios, neuroscientist — through the founding of DeepMind in 2009, the AlphaGo and AlphaFold milestones, and his current thinking on the path to AGI. He discusses AI for science, simulations as a new frontier, consciousness, his philosophical influences (Kant, Spinoza), and gives a rare rapid-fire timeline: AGI by 2030.

## Key Claims / Takeaways
- **AGI timeline: 2030**. Hassabis says he has been "pretty consistent about that." DeepMind's 2009 founding pitch was a "20 year mission"; he says the field is "basically exactly on track."
- **DeepMind founding thesis (2009)**: Combine deep learning (just invented by Hinton) with reinforcement learning — two siloed academic fields. Almost no one else had tried this combination. Also betting on accelerated compute (GPUs → TPUs).
- **Original mission statement**: "Step one: solve intelligence, i.e. build AGI. Step two: use it to solve everything else."
- **AlphaFold**: Described as a 50-year grand challenge. AI for science formally started the day after the AlphaGo Seoul match (~10 years ago). Led by Pushmeet Kohli.
- **Isomorphic Labs**: DeepMind spin-out building adjacent drug-discovery technology — designing compounds that bind to proteins identified by AlphaFold. Goal: reduce drug discovery from ~10 years to months, then weeks or days. Near-term drug discovery revolution predicted within "next few years."
- **Drug discovery dream**: 99% of exploration done in silico; wet lab reserved for validation only.
- **Simulations as new science**: AI-powered simulators could transform emergent-system fields (economics, biology) that currently can't run controlled experiments. "Machine learning is the perfect description language for biology in the same way maths is for physics."
- **WeatherNext**: DeepMind's weather simulation model, described as the most accurate in the world and far faster than meteorologist-used models.
- **Virtual cell**: Project to build a simulated biological cell — a complex emergent system.
- **Information-first cosmology**: Hassabis's personal theoretical view — information is more fundamental than energy and matter; E=mc² analogy extended to information. AI is fundamentally information processing.
- **Consciousness**: Views it as requiring self-awareness, concept of self-and-other, and temporal continuity — necessary but not sufficient conditions. Discusses Daniel Dennett (recently deceased at time of recording). Argues substrate equivalence is why we don't debate each other's consciousness, but AI will always lack substrate equivalence.
- **Classic Turing machine sufficiency**: AlphaFold demonstrates a classical system can model quantum biological systems to near-optimal approximation. Many quantum-seeming problems may be solvable classically.
- **Favorite book**: The Fabric of Reality by David Deutsch — wants to answer its questions using AGI.
- **Game history**: Theme Park (Bullfrog, age ~17; 10M+ copies sold); Elixir Studios founded straight after college; Republic (full country simulation on Pentium) — too ambitious, important lesson about being "5 years ahead not 50."
- **Philosopher influences**: Kant ("the mind creates reality"), Spinoza (deterministic/spiritual dimension of scientific inquiry).
- **John von Neumann**: Hassabis's choice as an ideal team member for high-stakes strategy games — "best game theorist."
- **Agent era**: Acknowledges we are "in the midst of the agent era now" — tool-first approach to AGI before crossing into agency/consciousness questions.
- **AI for science group**: Formally led by Pushmeet Kohli; has existed for nearly a decade at DeepMind.
