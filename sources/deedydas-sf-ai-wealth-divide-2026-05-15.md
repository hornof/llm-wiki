## Summary

Deedy Das (@deedydas) X post May 15 2026 on the **sentiment/cultural state of San Francisco** under the current AI-capital concentration: *"The vibes in SF feel pretty frenetic right now. The divide in outcomes is the worst I've ever seen."* Names four converging effects — (1) corporate-ladder-feels-wrong career anxiety, (2) malaise about work and its future, (3) middle-manager paralysis, (4) post-economic founder lack-of-purpose. Cross-references the [[ai-labor-market-impacts]] structural framework from the sentiment side: an essay rather than a paper, but adds first-person cultural texture to the "young-worker hiring slowdown" data the Anthropic / Brynjolfsson framework captures.

## Key Claims / Takeaways

### The wealth-concentration claim

- *"Over the last 5yrs, a group of ~10k people — employees at Anthropic, OpenAI, xAI, Nvidia, Meta TBD, founders — have hit retirement wealth of well above $20M (back of the envelope AI estimation)."* — Treat as Deedy's estimate, not a measured figure. The named cohort (frontier-lab employees + their adjacent equity holders) is the right shape; the specific count and threshold are anecdotal.
- **Structural framing**: *"Everyone outside that group feels like they can work their well-paying (but <$500k) job for their whole life and never get there."* — the **discontinuous-outcome distribution** is the load-bearing claim, not the specific numbers.

### The four converging effects

1. **"The corporate ladder looks like the wrong building to climb."** — Career-path re-alignment toward founder / frontier-lab / equity-bet. *"People are demanding higher salaries and switching jobs more and more."*
2. **"There's a deep malaise about work (and its future)."** — *"Why even work at all for 'peanuts'? Will my job even exist in a few years? Many feel helpless. You hear the 'permanent underclass' conversation a lot, esp from young people."*
3. **"The mid to late middle managers feel paralyzed."** — *"Many have families and don't feel like they have the energy or network to just 'start a company'. They don't particularly have any AI skills. They see the writing on the wall: middle management is being hollowed out."* — Cross-confirms the [[fastcompany-amazon-workers-ai-task-faking-2026-05|Amazon workers gaming AI utilization]] failure mode at the cultural-sentiment layer.
4. **"The rich aren't particularly happy either."** — *"Some have gone from <$150k to >$50M in a few years with no ramp. It flips your life plans upside down... they start companies 'just cuz', often to win status points... 'and do what? right now, everyone wants to talk to me. if i sell, I will only have money.'"*

### The closing irony

*"Ironically, a frequent side effect of this torment is to spin up the very products making everyone rich in hopes that you too can vibecode your path to economic enlightenment."* — Names the **vibecoding-as-escape-hatch** cultural pattern. Pairs with [[vibe-coding]] (Karpathy's hobbyist-mode framing) and [[scobleizer-ai-native-companies]] (Scoble's "build the AI-native company before the next guy does" framing).

### Comment-thread payload

- **Robert Scoble**: *"So few kept their skills up to date and then get rolled over. Even fewer figure out where the puck is going and try to get ahead. But the changes are happening so fast people struggle to keep up."* — Cross-confirms #3 (middle-manager paralysis) from the perspective of a longtime SF tech observer.
- **Hot Aisle**: *"None of this is new."* — counter-take: dot-com / mobile / crypto cycles produced similar discontinuous outcome distributions.

## Why It Matters

- **First wiki capture of the cultural-sentiment side of [[ai-labor-market-impacts]]**. The Anthropic / Brynjolfsson / Korinek work covers the measurement and timeline frames; Deedy's essay captures the **lived-experience texture** that the structural papers don't try to measure. Useful as a sentiment-side companion to the data-side framework.
- **Vibecoding-as-escape-hatch framing is novel and load-bearing**: the cultural logic by which AI-anxiety becomes AI-adoption is the same loop that produces the [[fastcompany-amazon-workers-ai-task-faking-2026-05|Goodhart-Law gaming]] under top-down mandates. Different driver (cultural vs corporate), same shape (AI-utilization as identity-protection).
- **Middle-manager paralysis is the same population** that [[benln-dorsey-mini-agi-2026-05-13|FundamentalAnalysis named as "first to atrophy"]] in the org-chart-flattening framing. Deedy's first-person texture and FundamentalAnalysis's structural argument converge on the same conclusion from different starting points.
- **Counter-take to track**: Hot Aisle's *"None of this is new"* — worth holding alongside the AI-discontinuity framing. Historical cycles (dot-com, mobile) produced similar outcome distributions; whether AI is structurally different or just the same shape at larger scale is an open question.

## Caveats

- **Anecdotal / first-person essay**, not a measurement. Specific numbers ($20M retirement wealth, 10k people, <$500k baseline) are Deedy's estimates.
- **SF-centric framing**. The "tech bubble" caveat in Deedy's own words: *"What is often well-off anywhere else in the world is bang average here."* Worth weighing against geographic generalizability.
- **No remediation framing**. Deedy describes the texture but doesn't claim a fix. Treat as cultural data, not policy proposal.
