## Summary

[[craig-smith|Craig Smith]]'s *Eye on A.I.* podcast episode, 2026-05-15, with [[kris-lovejoy|Kris Lovejoy]] (Global Strategy Leader, [[kyndryl|Kyndryl]]). Lovejoy's diagnosis: *"agentic AI is a bullet train sitting on tracks built for 30 miles per hour. The technology is ready. Most organizations aren't, and the gap between a successful pilot and a production system running at scale is far wider than the hype suggests."* Concrete enterprise-readiness counter to the [[ai-native-organizations]] / [[ai-labor-market-impacts]] threads. Episode walks through (1) **IT service management as the smartest entry point** for agentic adoption, (2) up to **90% cost savings in ITSM** as the funding mechanism for broader modernization, (3) security risks reframed as **misconfiguration / bad context / human error**, not sophisticated attackers, (4) specific prediction: **half of traditional IT administration tasks handled by AI agents by 2031**, (5) surprising labor take: *"not coders, but people trained to ask the right questions"* will thrive in the agentic era.

## Key Claims / Takeaways

### The "bullet train on 30 mph tracks" framing

The headline metaphor — *"agentic AI is a bullet train sitting on tracks built for 30 miles per hour"* — is the cleanest articulation captured to date of the enterprise-readiness gap. Pairs structurally with [[benln-dorsey-mini-agi-2026-05-13|FundamentalAnalysis's "flatten before reliable" framing]] (org-chart restructuring outpaces AI capability) — both name the same shape, with Lovejoy operating from the *infrastructure-side* and FundamentalAnalysis from the *org-design side*.

### IT Service Management as the entry-point wedge

Lovejoy's strategic argument: **ITSM is the smartest first-line agentic deployment** for enterprises because (a) workflows are well-defined, (b) failure modes are bounded and observable, (c) **cost savings up to 90%** in ITSM produce the budget that funds broader modernization. The implied ROI loop: agents → ITSM cost reduction → modernization budget → broader agent deployment. Pairs with [[anthropic-finance-agents-2026-05-05|Anthropic's vertical finance agents]] (KYC screener, statement auditor, GL reconciler) as an instance of "back-office workflows have the right shape for early agentic deployment."

### Security framing: not hackers, configuration

Lovejoy reframes the agentic security risk: **less about sophisticated external attackers, more about misconfiguration, bad context, and human error**. Important calibration against [[google-criminal-hackers-ai-vuln-2026-05-11|the AI-vulnerability-discovery thread]] — Lovejoy's framing is the *defender-side* version: even when adversarial AI-vuln-discovery is real, the practical deployment risk is operator error, not attacker capability. Aligns with the [[theregister-ontario-clinical-ai-audit-2026-05-14|Ontario audit]] pattern: deployed-system failures are dominated by *systemic configuration issues* (vendor selection weighting accuracy at 4%) rather than capability gaps.

### The 2031 prediction

*"Half of traditional IT administration tasks will be handled by AI agents by 2031."* — concrete dated prediction; useful as a calibration anchor for the [[ai-labor-market-impacts]] framework. Falls in the middle of the timeline between (a) [[anthropic-labor-market-impacts-2026-03-05|Anthropic's current observation]] (no aggregate unemployment effect yet; 14% young-worker hiring slowdown in exposed occupations) and (b) the [[import-ai-456-clark-rsi-radical-optionality-2026-05-11|Korinek RSI]] full-automation-of-software-R&D scenarios (~6 years to explosive regime).

### The labor take

*"Not coders, but people trained to ask the right questions."* Direct contrast to the [[anthropic-labor-market-impacts-2026-03-05|Computer Programmers 75% covered]] finding — Lovejoy is arguing the *displacement* maps to current programmer roles but the *replacement* requires a different skill profile entirely. Adjacency to [[karpathy-vibe-coding-agentic-engineering|Karpathy's agentic engineering]] (engineering judgment moves up the stack to coordinating agents). Worth holding as a *counter-prediction* to the current-job-mapping framing of the labor-market thesis.

### The host

**Craig Smith** — long-time *New York Times* journalist, host of *Eye on A.I.* Each episode is a longform interview with a single AI practitioner/researcher. **First wiki capture** of Craig Smith and the Eye on A.I. podcast — a high-signal practitioner-interview surface separate from the [[latentspace-abridge-healthcare-os-2026-05-15|Latent Space]] tooling-focused and [[import-ai-456-clark-rsi-radical-optionality-2026-05-11|Import AI]] policy-synthesis lanes.

## Why It Matters

- **Calibration against the AI-native-org optimism.** Most wiki-tracked AI-native-org content ([[block]], [[sentra]], [[shopify]] / [[river]], [[anthropic-gates-foundation-200m-2026-05-14|Anthropic's institutional-distribution moves]]) skews bullish. Lovejoy is the principled enterprise-readiness counterweight: *the technology is ready; most organizations are not.* The pipe-to-production gap is the load-bearing claim.
- **ITSM as the unsexy beachhead is structurally important.** Vertical-AI coverage so far skews to high-prestige domains (finance, healthcare, legal). Lovejoy names *internal IT operations* as the actually-best-suited domain for early production deployment. Worth tracking against the [[anthropic-finance-agents-2026-05-05|finance-agents]] and [[latentspace-abridge-healthcare-os-2026-05-15|Abridge]] surfaces — back-office-IT may produce production deployments faster than the headline verticals.
- **Configuration-as-security pivot.** The framing that practical agentic security risk is dominated by misconfiguration and bad context (not attackers) maps to the [[concepts/verifiability-and-jagged-intelligence]] framework — system-level failure modes outpace capability-level failures in deployed environments.
- **The 2031 prediction is dated and bounded.** Useful as a falsifiable anchor — if 50% of traditional IT admin tasks aren't agent-handled by 2031, Lovejoy's framework is wrong; if they are, the labor-market-impacts framework needs to predict the cascade effects.
- **First wiki capture of the *Eye on A.I.* podcast** as a practitioner-interview surface. The host Craig Smith brings NYT-journalist disciplines (verification, push-back, context) that complement the more practitioner-style Latent Space format.
