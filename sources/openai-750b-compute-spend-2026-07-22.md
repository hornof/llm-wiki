## Summary

Per TechCrunch (2026-07-22), **[[openai|OpenAI]]'s planned infrastructure spend has reached ~$750B through 2030** — *"some 25% more than it estimated earlier this year."* Concrete new build: **Project Camellia**, a **$20B data-center campus in Georgia** (1,400 acres, drawing *"at least 3.2 gigawatts of power"*). Notably, the flagship **Stargate project is described as having "stalled."** A capital-intensity datapoint feeding the [[ai-roi-gap|AI-ROI-gap]] / capex-overhang thread. *(Primary fetched — the article is thin on breakdowns.)*

## Key Claims / Takeaways

- **~$750B on infrastructure through 2030** — a **25% increase** over OpenAI's own earlier-2026 estimate. (Absolute prior figure not disclosed; framed as compute/data-center infrastructure, not split capex/opex.)
- **Project Camellia**: a **$20B, 1,400-acre data-center campus** northwest of Savannah, GA, drawing **≥3.2 GW** of power.
- **Stargate "stalled"** — the marquee OpenAI compute-buildout project is flagged as stalled (no detail on why in this piece).
