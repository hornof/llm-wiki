## Key Claims / Takeaways

### What the brief captures

- **Willison's thesis**: Anthropic and OpenAI have both found PMF, but with **different offerings** (the brief doesn't surface what the two distinct PMFs are — primary fetch required).
- **Brief framing — hot take**: *"PMF doesn't mean the market's won. It means the API tax got cheap enough that builders stopped waiting for better. Now the real product work starts."*
- **Brief framing — insightful**: *"PMF for APIs ≠ PMF for end-user products. Anthropic and OpenAI solved the model-delivery problem; they haven't solved whether anyone needs the thing the model is *in*. That's the next 18 months."*

### The API-PMF vs end-user-PMF distinction

The brief's "insightful" framing draws a sharp line:

- **API PMF (solved)**: builders integrate frontier models at scale; the cost-curve and quality-curve crossed the threshold where waiting-for-better is dominated by build-with-current.
- **End-user product PMF (open)**: whether anyone needs the *thing the model is in* (the surface, the workflow, the product). This is the next 18 months.

**Pairs with [[topics/saas-disruption-thesis|SaaS-disruption thesis]] in a structural way** — the question of "do end-user products around frontier models have PMF" is the question of whether the SaaS layer above frontier models is durable or disrupted.
