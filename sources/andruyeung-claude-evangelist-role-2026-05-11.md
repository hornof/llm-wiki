## Summary

Andrew Yeung (@andruyeung) X post (May 11 2026) surfacing [[anthropic]]'s open role for a **Claude Evangelist** — up to **$315K base** — described as a "Head of IRL" role embedded in the startup ecosystem (founders, VCs, accelerators) to host events and produce content driving Claude awareness and adoption. The defining requirement Yeung highlights: the role demands *both* technical depth *and* exceptional in-person / public-speaking skills. Light cultural-signal ingest — no entity page for Yeung created; he is the surfacing voice, not the subject.

## Key Claims / Takeaways

- **Hybrid skill profile is the explicit ask**: "They have to be the hybrid athlete of the AI era — excellent behind a screen and excellent in person." Yeung's framing: in a world flooded with AI content, the companies that win are those with the most compelling *human* voices.
- **Compensation point**: up to $315K base. Practitioner comment Louis Amira: "Comp should be 10x higher. This person is worth $5-10M to them. Probably $100M if they're the actual face of it." Yeung's reply: "Sounds aggressive." Useful as a soft-cap data point on what Anthropic publicly anchors for an ecosystem-facing senior role.
- **Diffusion-path gap noted in comments** (Jonathan Guy / @PointWake25): "Startup ecosystem evangelism is the visible play. The trades-operator world (HVAC, dispatch, plumbing) has no equivalent role. No Claude Evangelist showing up at fleet conferences. The diffusion path for that segment is still missing." Relevant to the [[saas-disruption-thesis]] / [[ai-native-organizations]] discussion of where adoption lands first.
- **Practitioner counterpoint** (Gregor / @bygregorr): "The face of Claude in the startup world is already every dev who ships with it daily. Does a hired evangelist actually move founders more than organic builders do?" Yeung's reply: "The evangelist needs to be a dev." Confirms the role is *not* a non-technical marketing hire.
- **Tone of practitioner reception**: mixed. Mockery from one quarter (@AIToolsBrief: "AI companies spent years telling us humans are getting replaced; now Anthropic's paying $315K for a human to go room to room convincing people to use their AI"), enthusiasm from others (some commenters asking about applying).
