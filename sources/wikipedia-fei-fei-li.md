## Summary
Wikipedia article on Fei-Fei Li — Stanford CS professor, creator of ImageNet, co-director of Stanford HAI, founder of AI4ALL and World Labs. One of the most influential figures in modern AI, particularly for enabling the deep learning revolution through large-scale datasets.

## Key Claims / Takeaways
- Created ImageNet (2007): 14M+ labeled images, 22,000 categories, built with Amazon Mechanical Turk — the dataset that catalyzed the deep learning revolution in computer vision
- Professor at Stanford; co-director of Stanford HAI (Human-Centered AI Institute)
- Chief Scientist of AI/ML at Google Cloud 2017–2018; developed AutoML
- Co-founded AI4ALL (2017) with Melinda French Gates and Jensen Huang — nonprofit for diversity in AI education
- Named Time 100 AI Most Influential People (2023); "Architects of AI" for Time Person of the Year (2025)
- Queen Elizabeth Prize for Engineering (2025) for role in advancing deep learning
- Founded World Labs (2024): "spatial intelligence" AI — reasoning in 3D physical environments; raised $230M seed, then $1B (2026)
- On AI governance (Feb 2025): "AI governance should be based on science rather than science fiction"
- Has been on partial leave from Stanford since Jan 2024 to focus on World Labs
- Board of Twitter (2020–2022); removed when Musk acquired the company
