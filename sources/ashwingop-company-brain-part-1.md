## Summary

Longform X post by [[ashwingop]] (Ashwin Gopalan, founder of [[sentra]]) defining the **Company Brain** thesis after YC's April 2026 RFS named the same opportunity. Ashwin claims the underlying product (Sentra) but acknowledges the name is YC's framing — "it is hard to hold a precise idea before it has a name, and sometimes simplicity travels faster than accuracy." Argues that companies accumulate fragments faster than they turn them into memory; that the missing substrate beneath agentic AI is *organizational memory*, not connected data; and that a Company Brain is a three-layer system (factual memory, context graph/reasoning, action coordination) plus the integration of human communication.

Heavily cited piece: links to Paul Graham (founder mode), Walsh & Ungson (organizational memory research), McKinsey (agentic AI foundations), Granola, Otter, TechCrunch, Glean, Zapier, ServiceNow, Dust. Personal frame: Ashwin's daughter Satakshi was born a week after Sentra was incorporated; her cognitive development mirrors how companies should accumulate fragments → memory → model → metacognition.

## Key Claims / Takeaways

- **Coordination is the underlying problem, not agents.** "This is not an agent problem first. It is a human coordination problem." AI makes the problem more visible by speeding up work while shared context stays fragile.
- **Founder mode = contact with company truth.** Ashwin's extension of Paul Graham: "customer pain, product tradeoffs, unresolved commitments, and weak signals before they become metrics."
- **Definition**: a Company Brain is "a living, permissioned model of how an organization remembers, reasons, and acts."
- **Three layers**:
  1. **Factual memory** — what happened, with provenance and permissions; most attempts stop here and become enterprise search with better branding.
  2. **Context graph / reasoning** — facts as a model of the company; this is also where **metacognition** lives (knowing when evidence is weak, context stale, assumptions conflict, commitments lack owners).
  3. **Action coordination** — drafts follow-ups from commitments, creates tickets from recurring complaints, warns about cross-team assumption drift; **distinct from automation**: "automation executes a known workflow. A company brain coordinates action from context."
- **Companies forget in strange ways.** Not just facts, but *why a fact mattered*, the argument that led to a decision, the counterfactuals, and the dissenting view that later turned out to be right.
- **Meeting notes alone are not the durable category.** Once macOS likely ships Granola-style transcription by default, meeting-note companies need a layer above transcription. The prize is "turning human interactions into organizational memory."
- **Adjacent categories converging on the same center**: knowledge tools (what exists) + meeting tools (what was said) + workflow tools (how to act) + agent tools (how to attempt tasks). Sentra's bet is that the durable position is the integration point.
- **The integration equation**: Factual memory + human communication + context graph and reasoning + governed action = Company Brain. Missing any one yields something useful but incomplete.
- **Architecture question**: aggregation (connect existing tools — easier for large companies with already-scattered context) vs. vertical integration (young company adopts memory, reasoning, action as part of OS from day one). Ashwin: "I don't know which architecture wins, but companies that start earlier will have an advantage."
- **Multi-role brain**: serves IC, manager, CEO, and agents at different levels of abstraction. If only for leadership → "surveillance with better UX"; if only for individuals → personal assistant, not organizational memory.
- **Citations to support**: Walsh & Ungson on organizational memory ("memory brought to bear on decisions"), transactive memory on "who knows what," McKinsey on agentic-AI foundations, OLK5 review on organizational learning.
- **Sentra's positioning** (final paragraph, lightly promotional): "enterprise general intelligence: a shared intelligence/memory layer that sits on all communication channels, knowledge bases and agent traces… constructing a living world model of the entire company in near real time."
