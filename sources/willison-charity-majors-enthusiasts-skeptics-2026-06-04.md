## Key Claims / Takeaways

### The framing

> ***"AI enthusiasts are in a race against time, AI skeptics are in a race against entropy."*** — Charity Majors

**Pattern**: enthusiasts and skeptics have *different binding constraints*, both correct.
- **Enthusiasts → time** — *windows close* (capability frontier moves; competitive advantage is in the early-adopter window; *temporally correct*)
- **Skeptics → entropy** — *systems degrade without maintenance* (technical debt accumulates; AI-generated code carries hidden complexity; *structurally correct*)

### Brief framings (Luke-side)

- *hot_take*: *"Enthusiasts racing time; skeptics racing entropy. Both right. The real problem: most teams have neither — just people trying not to look stupid while the ground shifts."*
- *insightful*: *"The frame inverts the usual 'adoption curve' story. It's not about convincing skeptics; it's about skeptics being structurally correct (systems degrade without maintenance) while enthusiasts are temporally correct (windows close). Orgs lose when they pick one over the other instead of running both in parallel."*
