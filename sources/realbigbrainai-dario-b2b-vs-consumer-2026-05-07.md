## Summary

Third-party amplification (May 7 2026) of a Dario Amodei quote on why [[anthropic]] chose enterprise customers over consumer scale. Dario frames the decision as **structural, not moral** — sidestepping engagement-maximization and ad-driven monetization is easier than fighting incentives once they exist. The choice keeps Anthropic's revenue tied to the product getting *better*, not more *addictive*.

## Key Claims / Takeaways

- **The structural argument**: *"It's very hard to fight your own business incentives. It's easier to choose a business model where there's less need to fight your own business incentives."*
- **Dario's worry about consumer AI**:
  > "I have a lot of worries about consumer AI that it kind of leads to needing to maximize engagement. It leads to slop. We've seen a lot of stuff around ads from some of the other players."
- **Why B2B sidesteps the trap**:
  > "Anthropic is not a player that works like that or needs to work like that. We just sell things to businesses and those things directly have value. We don't need to monetize a billion free users. We don't need to maximize engagement for a billion free users because we're in some death race with some other large player."
- **Crucial framing**: not a virtue claim. Dario is pointing out that **consumer business models force engagement-maximization regardless of what leaders personally want**. The structural choice replaces the willpower problem.
- **Connects to Anthropic's vertical-build pattern**: the May 2026 vertical-product wave (Claude for Financial Services, the Anthropic enterprise services JV) is the operational expression of this same B2B-first stance — every shipped vertical is an enterprise product, no consumer-focused launch in the May 2026 cluster.
