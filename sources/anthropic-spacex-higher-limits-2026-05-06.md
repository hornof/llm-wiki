## Summary

Anthropic primary announcement (May 6, 2026) bundling two events: (1) raised Claude usage limits across paid tiers and the API, and (2) a compute partnership with **SpaceX** under which Anthropic gains access to **over 300 MW of new compute capacity (220,000+ NVIDIA GPUs) at Colossus 1**, available "within the month." Anthropic also states future interest in "partnering with SpaceX to develop multiple gigawatts of **orbital AI compute capacity**." This is the Anthropic-primary capture of the same deal [[simon-willison]] reacted to one day later via [[willison-anthropic-xai-colossus-2026-05]] — the corporate-parent framing is now SpaceX rather than [[xai]], consistent with [[forbes-ai-50-2026]]'s prior note that xAI was acquired by SpaceX and now operates as its AI subsidiary.

## Key Claims / Takeaways

- **Usage limit changes (Claude Code)**: five-hour rate limits doubled on **Pro, Max, Team, and Enterprise**; the peak-hours limit reduction is **removed** on Pro and Max.
- **Usage limit changes (API)**: rate limits on Claude Opus models raised "considerably" — specific figures shown in a table on the announcement page (not extracted verbatim here).
- **No free-tier changes** mentioned.
- **Compute partnership scope**: SpaceX provides Anthropic with **all compute capacity at Colossus 1** (the Memphis-area data center previously associated with xAI / Grok), a capacity expansion described as **300+ MW / 220,000+ NVIDIA GPUs**.
- **Timing**: capacity coming online "within the month" — i.e., May 2026.
- **Forward-looking signal**: Anthropic explicitly names **orbital AI compute capacity** as a multi-gigawatt direction it is exploring with SpaceX. First wiki-tracked instance of a frontier-lab compute roadmap line that crosses the atmosphere.
- **Corporate-parent reconciliation**: Anthropic's "SpaceX" framing and Willison's "xAI/Colossus" framing describe the same physical facility and the same commercial relationship. xAI was acquired by SpaceX in 2025 ([[xai]], [[forbes-ai-50-2026]]); Colossus 1 is operated under the SpaceX corporate umbrella as a result. Anthropic's announcement uses the parent-company name; Willison's reaction used the operator-name still familiar to the AI community.
