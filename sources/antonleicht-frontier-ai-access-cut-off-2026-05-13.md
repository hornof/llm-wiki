## Summary

Anton Leicht essay on *Threading the Needle* (Substack), published 2026-05-13. **Core thesis: frontier-AI access is becoming scarce through three compounding gates** — (1) security-driven restrictions on misuse, (2) economic compute scarcity, (3) US-government strategic gatekeeping. The piece argues this moves beyond temporary precaution toward *structural* gatekeeping that rivals industrial-revolution-scale inequality between AI haves and have-nots. Names [[anthropic|Anthropic]]'s **Mythos cybersecurity model limited to select US firms**, **OpenAI's similar Daybreak initiative**, the **GAIN Act** proposal (US right-of-first-refusal for American chips), and **NSA predeployment review** as concrete mechanisms. First wiki capture of Leicht's voice — paired with [[jack-clark]]'s [[import-ai-456-clark-rsi-radical-optionality-2026-05-11|"radical optionality"]] framing as the policy-analyst external counterpart to Clark's lab-side framing.

## Key Claims / Takeaways

### The three gates

1. **Security-driven access restrictions** — model-specific restrictions on misuse and dual-use concerns
2. **Economic compute scarcity** — frontier models require massive computational resources with high marginal per-token cost; unlike software (low distribution cost), AI tokens remain expensive to serve, making broad access economically disadvantageous relative to selective deployment
3. **US-government strategic gatekeeping** — access weaponized as foreign-policy leverage

### Named mechanisms and labs

- **Anthropic Mythos** ([[claude-mythos]]) — preview-access cybersecurity model "limited to select US firms" (matches the [[willison-firefox-claude-mythos-2026-05|Mozilla / Firefox security partnership]] pattern; Leicht generalizes it to a category)
- **OpenAI Daybreak** — analogous selective-deployment cybersecurity initiative (first wiki capture; not previously surfaced)
- **GAIN Act** — US legislative proposal: right-of-first-refusal for American chips
- **NSA predeployment review** — process layer for restricted-deployment frontier models
- **KYC restrictions** on frontier-model access
- **xAI / SpaceX compute partnership** ([[anthropic-spacex-higher-limits-2026-05-06]]) cited as compute-side example

### Distillation as sustained-restriction driver

Distinction Leicht makes: security concerns drive *initial* restrictions, but **distillation risk** — where competitors (named: **DeepSeek**) extract capabilities through API access — motivates *sustained* constraints. The economic argument is downstream of this: even if costs fall, the *risk model* keeps the access gate up.

Timeline: **"6-9 months"** for distillation lag; models become cheaper "next year" but frontier capabilities grow **"more expensive month-to-month for years now."**

### Jurisdictional layering

- **US** — domestic priority
- **Europe** — explicitly disadvantaged
- **China** — competitive threat
- **Non-US allies** — encouraged to negotiate compute infrastructure for frontier-access guarantees (subsidized energy exchange)
- **Middle powers** — should retain contingency capabilities for worst-case scenarios

### Recommendations

1. Harden world infrastructure to reduce security-driven restriction pressure
2. Deploy technical distillation fixes **before** government mandates heavier controls
3. Rapidly expand global datacenter capacity
4. Non-US allies negotiate compute-for-frontier-access trades with the US
5. Middle powers maintain sovereign-AI contingency

### Headline quote

> "AI tokens will be strategically and economically central to all future societies, so we should do our best to enable their free flow."

## Why It Matters

- **Names a structural pattern the wiki has been tracking in fragments.** [[claude-mythos]] / [[willison-firefox-claude-mythos-2026-05]] surfaced selective-deployment as a single-vendor pattern. Leicht generalizes: every frontier lab is converging on the *same* selective-deployment posture, and the convergence is the policy signal. OpenAI Daybreak as a named parallel to Anthropic Mythos is the cleanest first-time confirmation of this generalization.
- **Distillation-driven sustained restriction** is the load-bearing argument. If access restriction were *only* security-driven, falling-cost dynamics would reopen the gate. Leicht's claim is that the distillation-risk model keeps the gate up indefinitely — *regardless* of cost trajectory. Important for any "AI will commoditize" thesis evaluation.
- **GAIN Act + NSA predeployment review** are concrete US-policy infrastructure that doesn't surface in horizontal frontier-lab coverage. Useful to track if VPE-evaluation conversations touch government-adjacent AI work.
- **Compute-for-frontier-access trade framing** is the kind of geopolitical primitive worth naming in advance. Wiki owner's Canada / Northern Europe career-context relevance (energy + dataset surplus + frontier-AI dependency) makes this a high-signal piece for any cross-border AI-applied-company evaluation.
- **Counterpoint to Anthropic's [[anthropic-gates-foundation-200m-2026-05-14|Gates Foundation $200M]]** and the [[anthropic-claude-platform-aws-2026-05|Claude Platform on AWS]] moves: those are *distribution-expansion* signals; Leicht is naming a *distribution-restriction* counterforce operating simultaneously at the frontier tier. Both can be true: distribution expands at the deployed-model tier while frontier-tier access contracts.
