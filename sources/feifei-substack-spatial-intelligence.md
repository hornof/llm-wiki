## Summary
Fei-Fei Li's primary public statement on spatial intelligence as the next frontier of AI. Argues that LLMs are "wordsmiths in the dark" — linguistically capable but physically blind. Proposes world models (generative, multimodal, interactive) as the path to machines that truly understand the physical world. Frames spatial intelligence as foundational to cognition itself, not just a useful capability.

## Key Claims / Takeaways
- **LLMs as "wordsmiths in the dark"**: eloquent but inexperienced; state-of-the-art multimodal models "rarely perform better than chance on estimating distance, orientation, and size"
- **Spatial intelligence as cognitive foundation**: "the core loop driving the evolution of intelligence" is perception and action; "Spatial Intelligence is the scaffolding upon which our cognition is built"
- **AGI requires spatial intelligence**: "AGI will not be complete without spatial intelligence" (Bloomberg, Nov 2025)
- **World models as the solution**: generative (physically consistent simulated worlds) + multimodal (images, text, depth maps, gestures) + interactive (predicts next world states from actions)
- **Scale of the challenge**: "exceeds anything AI has faced before" — physical laws are harder than language; requires reconciling semantic, geometric, dynamic, and physical dimensions simultaneously
- **AI cannot navigate mazes, recognize shortcuts, or predict basic physics** — specific failure modes of current systems
- **Human-centered framing**: "AI must augment human capability, not replace it"
- **World Labs Marble** (Nov 2025): world-generating AI model that reconstructs, generates, and simulates 3D worlds
