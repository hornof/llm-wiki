## Summary
Twitter thread by @MilkRoadAI summarizing Demis Hassabis's (CEO, Google DeepMind) definition of AGI and his estimate of when it arrives. Hassabis's definition is stricter than most in the industry; he calls current systems "jagged intelligence" and estimates AGI is 5–10 years away, contingent on 1–2 fundamental breakthroughs beyond scaling.

## Key Claims / Takeaways
- **Hassabis's AGI definition**: "A system that can exhibit all the cognitive capabilities humans can" — stricter than most CEO definitions
- **Why neuroscience**: the human brain is "the only confirmed existence proof that general intelligence is even possible" — he studied it to understand the target
- **Jagged intelligence**: current systems spike brilliantly in one area and collapse on similar problems if framed differently — not general intelligence
- **What's missing**: true creativity, continual learning, and long-term planning; today's systems are trained then frozen
- **The Einstein Test**: train on all human knowledge cut off at 1911 — can the system independently discover general relativity (as Einstein did by 1915)? Current models cannot pass it
- **AGI timeline**: 5–10 years away; requires 1–2 fundamental breakthroughs beyond scaling — specifically continual learning, efficient memory, and long-term reasoning
- **Scale of impact**: "10x the Industrial Revolution, at 10x the speed, unfolding over a decade"
- **Credibility signal** (@Soroosh_Tajdar): "Every AI lab CEO has a definition of AGI that conveniently makes their own product look closer to it. Hassabis's definition makes DeepMind look further away. That alone makes him more credible."
