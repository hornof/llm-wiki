## Summary

Forbes' eighth annual AI 50 list spotlights the most promising privately-held AI companies in the world as of April 2026. OpenAI and Anthropic together have raised $242.6 billion — roughly 80% of the $305.6 billion total raised by all 50 companies. The list includes 20 newcomers and reflects both consolidation (Scale AI, xAI, Windsurf absorbed by acquirers) and explosive growth in coding agents, drug discovery, robotics, and open-source model development. See also the companion [[ai-50-brink-list-2026|AI 50 Brink List]] for early-stage companies just below the cut.

## Key Claims / Takeaways

- **Anthropic**: $60B total funding; revenue $4.5B in 2025; Claude surpassed ChatGPT as most downloaded app on App Store (February 2026); valued at $380B. Pentagon blacklisted Anthropic after it refused to budge on safeguards around autonomous weapons and mass domestic surveillance.
- **OpenAI**: $182.6B total funding; annualized revenue >$25B as of February 2026; Codex product competing in the coding market.
- **Combined**: OpenAI + Anthropic = $242.6B of the list's $305.6B total.
- **Cursor**: valued at $29.3B; coding agent competing against Anthropic's Claude Code and OpenAI's Codex.
- **Reflection**: 2-year-old Brooklyn startup; $2.1B raised, valued at $8B; building open-source models to compete with DeepSeek; backed by Nvidia, Sequoia, Lightspeed, 1789 Capital; founded by former Google DeepMind researchers Misha Laskin and Ioannis Antonoglou (led early Gemini work); has not publicly released its own AI yet; building a data center in South Korea.
- **World Labs**: $1B raised; listed as spatial AI model developer; founded 2023; HQ San Francisco.
- **Physical Intelligence**: $1B raised; training foundational models for robots using human teleoperator data; HQ San Francisco; founded 2024.
- **Mistral AI**: $3.1B raised; open-weight models sold to large corporations like Cisco and European government agencies; valued at $14B (per accompanying Forbes feature); HQ Paris; founded 2023.
- **Safe Superintelligence**: $3B raised; AI research; HQ Palo Alto; founded 2024.
- **Thinking Machines Lab**: $2B raised; led by former OpenAI CTO Mira Murati; female-led; founded 2025.
- **Databricks**: $20B raised; data storage and analytics; founded 2013.
- **Cognition**: $1B raised; AI coding agents; valued at $10B; made debut this year; acquired the remaining Windsurf assets after Google paid $2.4B to hire Windsurf's cofounders and license its technology.
- **xAI**: acquired by SpaceX creating a combined entity worth $1.25 trillion (no longer independently on the list).
- **Scale AI**: CEO Alexandr Wang left to start Meta's superintelligence lab; company remains independent.
- **Gamma**: $91M raised; valued at $2.1B; profitable since 2023; 100M users total, 600K regular paying users ($9–$100/month).
- **Suno**: $375M raised; music generation; Cambridge, MA.
- **Midjourney**: $0 external funding; image generation; bootstrapped.
- **Harvey**: $1B raised; legal automation.
- **Perplexity**: $1.7B raised; AI search engine.
- **ElevenLabs**: $800M raised; voice generation.
- **SambaNova**: $1.5B raised; AI chipmaker.
- **Fireworks AI**: $327M raised; valued at $4B; female-led (CEO not named in main list); helps developers access models without backend complexity.
