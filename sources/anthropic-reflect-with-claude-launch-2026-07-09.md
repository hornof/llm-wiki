## Summary

**Anthropic** launches **Reflect** (beta, 2026-07-09; `anthropic.com/news/reflect-with-claude`) — a **usage-reflection dashboard** in Settings on Claude web + the [[claude-desktop|desktop app]] that visualizes *how* you use Claude (key topics, usage patterns, task types) over 1/3/6/12 months, invites periodic reflective prompts, and offers optional **quiet hours** + **break nudges**. It is explicitly framed against the **[4D AI-fluency framework](https://anthropic.skilljar.com/ai-fluency-framework-foundations)** (Delegation / Description / Discernment / Diligence) and co-developed with digital-wellbeing experts. First wiki-captured Anthropic **consumer-side "reflect on your own AI use" / digital-wellbeing product surface** — a vendor move distinct from the productivity/agentic-capability launches the wiki mostly tracks.

## Key Claims / Takeaways

### The feature
- **What it is**: a reflection dashboard — summary of how you've been using Claude (key topics, usage patterns, task types), with a breakdown of **when** you use Claude most and **what** you spent time on. A "how much time" view is promised "soon."
- **Lookback windows**: 1, 3, 6, or 12 months of chat activity.
- **Reflective prompts**: periodically surfaces questions like *"What's one thing you want to keep doing yourself, even if Claude could do it faster?"* and lets you talk it through with Claude.
- **Wellbeing controls**: set **quiet hours** or schedule a **nudge to take a break** after a set amount of usage — both framed as dismissible reminders of the user's own preferences.
- **4D AI-fluency mapping**: the report scores your activity across **Delegation** (deciding whether/how to engage AI), **Description** (prompting), **Discernment** (assessing AI output), **Diligence** (taking responsibility) — with concrete examples (e.g. "you often rework email drafts in your own voice," "you delegate only after settling the strategy yourself") and practical nudges (e.g. "start a Project instead of re-explaining context").

### Availability + privacy
- **Availability**: beta for **Free, Pro, and Max** users who have **[[claude-desktop|Memory]] turned on**; Settings → Reflect on web or desktop. **Cowork ([[claude-cowork]]) reflection "coming soon."**
- **Privacy design**: excludes **incognito chats**; does **not** pull underlying files from connected tools (a summarized inbox may appear, but not the source emails); **health-integration** conversations excluded entirely; insights not reused for other purposes. Sensitive conversations appear only "at a high level." Developed with the **MIT Media Lab AHA program**, the **Digital Wellness Lab at Boston Children's Hospital**, and the **Family Online Safety Institute**.

### Why it's notable
- **First wiki-captured Anthropic consumer-wellbeing / metacognition product surface** — orthogonal to the agentic-capability arc; it instruments the *human* side of the human-AI loop rather than adding autonomy.
- **The "keep doing yourself even if Claude could do it faster" prompt** rhymes with two threads the wiki already tracks: [[andrew-ng|Andrew Ng]]'s *"humans have a context advantage"* framing ([[andrew-ng-loop-engineering-3-loop-validation-2026-06-30]]) and the [[loop-engineering|loop-engineering]] closing caution to *"build it like someone who intends to stay the engineer."* Reflect is the vendor operationalizing that caution as a consumer feature.
- **Memory-gated**: ties the feature to Claude's Memory capability, reinforcing Memory as a substrate for personalization/insight features, not just recall.
