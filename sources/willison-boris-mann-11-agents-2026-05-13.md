## Summary

Simon Willison quote-collection, 2026-05-13, surfacing a Boris Mann Bluesky post that critiques the empty-signifier use of "AI agent" counts. Mann's quote: *"'11 AI agents' is meaningless as a phrase. If I said 'I have 11 spreadsheets' or 'I have 11 browser tabs' to do my work, it means about the same thing."* Original Mann post on Bluesky (`bsky.app/profile/bmann.ca/post/3mlp2ipupv22z`). Willison adds no editorial commentary — the amplification *is* the commentary. Short item but a useful terminology-hygiene marker for the wiki: "agent" as deployed count is not a meaningful unit, the same way "spreadsheet count" or "tab count" is not.

## Key Claims / Takeaways

- **Mann's verbatim claim:** "'11 AI agents' is meaningless as a phrase. If I said 'I have 11 spreadsheets' or 'I have 11 browser tabs' to do my work, it means about the same thing."
- **The analogy structure:** "agents" sits in the same category as spreadsheets and browser tabs — a count of containers that say nothing about what's inside them or what work they do. The number is decorative.
- **Why Willison surfaces it:** consistent with his broader 2026 stance on agent terminology (see [[people/simon-willison]] notable takes; his March 2026 "agents are a marketing term" thread, his ongoing distinction between "agentic workflow" and "agent count"). This is the cleanest one-line restatement of the position from a third party.
- **Boris Mann context:** Vancouver-based long-time open-web/Bluesky/Fission practitioner; not previously surfaced on this wiki. Bluesky handle `bmann.ca`. Worth a [[people/]] capture if the agent-terminology critique extends or if he surfaces on adjacent topics — for now, lighter touch via this source.
- **Pattern for the wiki:** when an entity-page traction-signals section says "X named agents" or "Y agent count," that's the marker Mann's critique flags as low-information. Future entries should describe agent *function* or *topology* (e.g., [[tools/river|River's shared-channel topology]]), not count.

## Why It Matters

- **Terminology hygiene:** the wiki captures lots of vendor announcements that lead with agent counts (e.g., [[anthropic-finance-agents-2026-05-05]] — "ten named pre-built agents"). Mann's critique doesn't invalidate the announcements but flags that the *count* is the wrong signal. What matters: what each agent does, what it's hooked into, what it refuses to do. The Anthropic finance-agents entry already captures this (six research-side, four ops-side; named integrations) — Mann's frame retroactively validates that structure.
- **For VPE evaluation:** a candidate or vendor pitch that leads with "we have 11 agents" should trigger the same skepticism as "we have 11 spreadsheets" — ask what each one does, what it connects to, and what would break if any one was removed.
- **Counterpoint to capture later:** there may be a legitimate sense in which agent *count* matters — e.g., maintenance overhead, observability surface, governance perimeter — but those framings should be made explicit when they apply, not assumed from the count alone.
