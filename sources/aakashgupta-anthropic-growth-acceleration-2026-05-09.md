## Summary

[[aakashgupta]] X post (May 9 2026) with concrete numbers on Anthropic's revenue trajectory and one of the cleanest single-frame articulations of the *"growth-rate acceleration past $10B ARR is structurally unprecedented in software history"* claim. Useful as a citation-grade snapshot of where Anthropic is positioned in the [[ai-50-2026-snapshot]] and the [[saas-disruption-thesis]] timeline.

## Key Claims / Takeaways

### Numbers stated

- Anthropic's growth rate is **accelerating** at $14B of scale (the curve is bending up on a log chart)
- **Dec 2022 → Jan 2025**: 10× per year, twice (i.e., compound 100×)
- **Dec 2025 → May 2026**: 5× in five months
- The annualized growth rate at $14B exceeds the annualized rate Anthropic posted at $1B
- **$1B → $44B ARR in seventeen months** (Slack held the previous SaaS record at $1B in five years)
- Salesforce's full FY2025 revenue: **$37.9B**. Anthropic's run-rate now **exceeds Salesforce's annual revenue**. Salesforce was founded in 1999.
- The most aggressive analyst projection from August 2025 had Anthropic at $30B ARR by August 2026 — **beat the bull case by four months**
- **Claude Code is the acceleration driver**. Launched May 2025. $2.5B in run-rate revenue by February 2026. Weekly active users doubled since January.
- Enterprise customers spending **>$1M/year went from 500 to 1,000 in two months** — *check size compounding, user count secondary*
- **The last ninety days added more ARR than the entire seventeen-month climb to $14B**
- Closing claim: *"There is no comparable curve in software history."*

### Why this is supposed to be impossible

> "Percentage growth decays as software companies scale. The largest software businesses have never sustained 50%+ YoY at $10B+ ARR, let alone accelerated."

### Notable practitioner pushback in the comments

- **Eshan Buildsworth (@eshanbuilds)**: *"\"no comparable curve in software history\" is doing a lot of work. AWS went from $0 to $85B in revenue while being underpriced relative to compute cost the entire time. anthropic is selling API tokens at prices that have fallen 90%+ per unit over the past 18[months]."* — important caveat: token price deflation means revenue acceleration ≠ value-per-token acceleration. The aggregate ARR number is still real but the "no comparable curve" claim deserves the AWS counterexample.
- **Dev Shah (@devlikesbizness)**: *"The scary part is not the number. It is that the number is speeding up. Most companies fight to keep growth flat at this scale. Anthropic is bending the curve the wrong way for everyone else."* — sharpens the structural-not-anecdotal framing.
