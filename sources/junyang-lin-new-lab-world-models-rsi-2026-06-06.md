**Pattern**: new lab focused at the intersection of **world models + RSI**, with thesis that **human-role shifts from engineering to supervision** as RSI compounds.

### Brief framings (Luke-side)

- *hot_take*: *"Recursive self-improvement shifts engineering from coding to supervision — but supervision of what? If the AI can't explain its own improvements, you're just managing a black box that gets faster at being opaque."*
- *insightful*: *"World models as the bridge between recursive self-improvement and interpretability. If self-improving systems learn to model the world first, supervision becomes tractable — you're checking *predictions*, not watching a loss curve descend into a singularity."*

### Lin's structural framing

**Lin's thesis = world models + RSI as a coupled research program**:
- **World models** make AI systems *legible* (their predictions can be checked against reality)
- **RSI** makes AI systems *capable of compounding* their own improvement
- **Coupled together**: RSI-with-world-models is *supervisable* in a way that pure RSI is not

This positions Lin as a **structural-bridge voice** between the world-models cluster ([[feifei-li-world-models-functional-taxonomy-2026-06-03|Fei-Fei taxonomy]], [[ami-labs|LeCun's AMI]], [[luma-ai|Luma's Uni-1]], [[world-labs|World Labs]]) and the RSI cluster ([[anthropic-institute-when-ai-builds-itself-2026-06-04|Anthropic Institute]], [[recursive-self-improvement|RSI concept]]).
