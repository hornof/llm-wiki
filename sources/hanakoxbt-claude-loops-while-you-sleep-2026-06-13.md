### Hanako's 5 canonical practitioner-discipline-articulations

**1. "A loop is just Claude on a schedule"** — canonical-simplicity articulation:
> *"You have Claude use cron to schedule a job, and you tell it how often to repeat: every minute, every 30 minutes, every night. That's it. No framework, no orchestration layer. The simplest thing that works, which is exactly why it works."*

**First wiki-captured "no framework, no orchestration layer" canonical-simplicity practitioner-tier framing** — counter-positions to LangChain + Vercel Eve + Omnigent framework-tier positioning at simplest-cron-only practitioner-tier.

**2. "Many loops at once" canonical scale-articulation**:
> *"One loop is useful. A stack of them running in parallel is a different thing entirely... This is how one person does the volume of a team."*

**First wiki-captured "one person does the volume of a team" canonical-Loop-Engineering-scale-anchor**. **First wiki-captured "what job should run on its own from now on" canonical-mental-shift framing** — counter-positions to "what do I prompt next" prompt-engineering-tier framing.

**3. "Close the laptop and the work keeps going" canonical-routines articulation**:
> *"The fix is routines, the same idea moved to the server. You configure the job once, and it runs on a schedule, a webhook, or an API call, whether your machine is on or not."*

**First wiki-captured "routines = loops moved to the server" canonical-architecture-distinction**. Pairs structurally with [[anthropic-dynamic-workflows-primary-2026-05-28|Anthropic Dynamic Workflows]] + [[sydney-runkle-langchain-4-layer-loop-engineering-2026-06-17|Sydney Runkle Event-driven loop (Level 3)]] at the **server-tier-operational-substrate canonical-architecture** layer.

**4. "Start with one loop, not ten" canonical-anti-pattern operator-discipline-articulation**:
> *"The mistake everyone makes is trying to build the whole system on day one. Ten loops, a dashboard, the works. It collapses by the weekend because you can't tell which loop did what... Start with one. Pick the most annoying recurring task you have."*

**First wiki-captured "start with one loop, not ten" canonical-anti-pattern operator-discipline-articulation**. **First wiki-captured "boring + bounded + easy-to-verify" canonical good-first-loop-properties triple-framing**:
- Runs on a clear schedule
- Has a narrow job it can't misread
- Output is something you can glance at in seconds

**Examples**: CI watcher + PR rebaser + daily digest. **First wiki-captured "Improve the codebase' is not a loop, it's a wish. 'Find functions over 50 lines and open an issue for each' is a loop" canonical-specificity-discipline framing**.

**5. "Babysit the rules not the work" canonical-operational-discipline framing**:
> *"They don't hand the agent a blank check. Each loop has a lane, and the risky steps still wait for a yes."*
>
> *"Set the boundaries once, in the loop's instructions, and they hold every run after. That's the whole trick: **you're not babysitting the work, you're babysitting the rules**, and the rules are written down."*

**First wiki-captured "babysit the rules not the work" canonical-operational-discipline framing**. **First wiki-captured "each loop has a lane" canonical-permission-discipline articulation**. **First wiki-captured "zero humans on the boring 95% + full attention on the 5% that actually carries risk" canonical-attention-discipline framing**.

> *"You're paying for a fleet of agents and using one chat window."*
