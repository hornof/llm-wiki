## Summary
Cognitive Revolution podcast episode with Luma AI CEO Amit Jain and co-founder Jiaming Song. Most technically detailed discussion of Luma's world model architecture. Covers their unified latent space thesis, Concepts framework, training dynamics, diffusion model evolution, and Jain's contrarian interpretability stance.

## Key Claims / Takeaways
- **Core thesis**: "Instead of language backbones retrofitting everything, we need a unified, singular latent space where we can think and reason about all different pieces of information as one." — Jain
- **Multimodality philosophy**: nature makes no distinction between modalities — all are signals from the same physical environment; current multimodal approaches (LLM + adapters) are inferior to dedicated vision models
- **Concepts framework**: scaffold to teach models new capabilities (camera motions, color grading, poses) from minimal examples; later internalize into base model
- **Training dynamics**: critical pathways form in the first 20-30K iterations; aggressive dataset filtering > scale
- **Blood-brain barrier**: Jain's term for the friction between users and the model's latent space; goal is to eliminate it
- **Diffusion evolution** (Jiaming Song): DDPM (2020) → classifier-free guidance → flow matching → inductive moment matching (distribution-level optimization)
- **Interpretability**: post-hoc interpretability is "archaeology"; productive interpretability = understanding training dynamics and dataset engineering
- **Video as the path to AGI**: storytelling requires reasoning and imagination that indicates genuine intelligence
