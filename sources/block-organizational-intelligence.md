## Summary

Essay (likely Sequoia-published, co-authored with Block leadership) tracing 2,000 years of organizational design history and arguing that AI has finally broken the underlying constraint — span of control — that has forced every large organization into hierarchy. Block describes its own restructuring around AI "world models" as the first real alternative to hierarchical information routing. Three roles replace traditional management: ICs, DRIs, and player-coaches.

## Key Claims / Takeaways

**Historical framing:**
- Roman contubernium (8 soldiers → 80 → 480 → 5,000) established span-of-control as the fundamental org constraint — every subsequent innovation works around it without solving it
- Prussian General Staff (post-1806) = first middle management: professionals whose job was information routing, not fighting
- Military hierarchy entered business via US railroads (1840s-50s); Daniel McCallum drew the first org chart to stop train collisions
- Failed post-hierarchy experiments: Spotify squads (reverted), Zappos Holacracy (attrition), Valve flat (didn't scale past ~400)

**Block's architecture:**
- **Company world model**: AI-maintained, continuously updated picture of all operations — replaces what middle managers used to do (routing context up and down)
- **Customer world model**: per-customer/merchant model built from transaction data (both sides: Cash App buyers + Square sellers); "money is the most honest signal in the world"
- **Intelligence layer**: composes atomic capabilities into proactive solutions; no product manager required; failure signals become the roadmap
- **Capabilities**: atomic financial primitives (payments, lending, card, banking) — no UIs, just reliability targets
- **Interfaces**: Square, Cash App, Afterpay, etc. — delivery surfaces, not where value is created

**Three new roles (replaces traditional hierarchy):**
- **ICs**: deep specialists; world model provides context that managers used to provide
- **DRIs (Directly Responsible Individuals)**: own cross-cutting problems/outcomes for a defined period; pull resources across teams; may be temporary
- **Player-coaches**: still build; also develop people; no status meetings, alignment sessions, or priority negotiations — world model handles those

**Core thesis**: "In a conventional company, the intelligence is spread throughout the people and the hierarchy routes it. In this model, the intelligence lives in the system. The people are on the edge."
