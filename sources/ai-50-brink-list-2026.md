## Summary

Forbes' inaugural AI 50 Brink List spotlights 20 early-stage (Seed and Series A) AI startups that are promising but just below the AI 50 cut. Published April 16, 2026. The 20 companies have raised over $3.5 billion in Seed/Series A funding combined. Average company age at time of listing: 24 months. This list is explicitly a "future pipeline" view — early signals of where the industry may go. See also the companion [[forbes-ai-50-2026|Forbes AI 50 2026]] for the main list.

## Key Claims / Takeaways

- **"Neo-labs" trend**: Multiple early executives from big labs (OpenAI, Meta, DeepMind, Anthropic) have departed to found new research-first ventures — a structural shift in how frontier research gets done.
- **Advanced Machine Intelligence (AMI Labs)**: cofounded by Yann LeCun and former senior Meta employees; raised over $1B since launch in 2026; building systems that learn from real-world data beyond text; targeting healthcare applications specifically. (Note: AMI Labs is tracked in the wiki as [[ami-labs]].)
- **Resolve AI**: recruits from Meta and DeepMind; identifies and fixes production code bugs; raised $40M Series A extension at $1.5B valuation.
- **Periodic Labs**: founded by Liam Fedus (ChatGPT co-creator); training models to accelerate scientific discovery in semiconductors, magnetism and superconductivity.
- **Humans&**: valued at $4.5B; founding team from Meta, OpenAI, Google DeepMind, and Anthropic; using AI to coordinate people and workflows.
- **Certuma**: building the first FDA-approved AI system capable of diagnosing conditions and prescribing treatments; CEO Martín Varsavsky.
- **Latent Health**: valued at $600M; AI agents that help doctors convince insurance providers to approve drugs faster.
- **Ricursive Intelligence**: CEO Anna Goldie; building self-improving AI chips; female-led.
- **Axiom**: CEO Carina Hong; developing an AI mathematician; female-led.
- **Nectar Social**: CEO Misbah Uraizee; monitoring social media creators' post-to-sales translation; female-led.
- **Accordance**: featured (CEO David Yue); no additional detail provided in source.
- **Flapping Airplanes**: founders Ben Spector, Asher Spector, and Aidan Smith; no additional detail provided in source.
- **Giga**: founder Varun Vummadi; no additional detail provided in source.
- **Irregular**: founder Dan Lahav; no additional detail provided in source.
- **Micro1**: founder Ali Ansari; no additional detail provided in source.
- **OpenRouter**: CEO Alex Atallah; LLM routing/access platform (inferred from name and context).
- **Rox**: founder Ishan Mukherjee; no additional detail provided in source.
- **Simile**: founder Joon Sung Park; no additional detail provided in source.
- **Tollbit**: founder Toshit Panigrahi; no additional detail provided in source.
- **WisprFlow**: founder Tanay Kothari; no additional detail provided in source.
- **Worktrace AI**: founder Deepak Vasisht; no additional detail provided in source.
- **Gender imbalance persists**: only 3 of 20 Brink companies are female-led (Ricursive Intelligence, Axiom, Nectar Social).
