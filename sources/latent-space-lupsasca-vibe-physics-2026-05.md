## Summary
Latent Space interviews Alex Lupsasca (OpenAI Science team; theoretical physicist; 2024 Breakthrough Prize in Fundamental Physics) about using GPT-5.x to derive genuinely novel results in theoretical physics — gluon amplitudes and graviton calculations that had stumped expert humans for over a year. Lupsasca coins **"vibe physics"** by direct analogy with [[vibe-coding]]: iterative dialogue with an AI that *extends the frontier of human knowledge* rather than automating known work. If verified by independent groups, this is a categorically different milestone than [[alphafold]]-style applications: novel frontier theory, not interpolation on a well-defined problem.

## Key Claims / Takeaways
- **Coined term**: "vibe physics" — iterative dialogue with an AI to derive new physics, by analogy with vibe coding. Distinguishing claim: "actually extending the frontier of human knowledge."
- **Gluon amplitudes**: GPT-5.2 derived results showing certain "single-minus gluon tree amplitudes" can be non-zero under specific conditions, contradicting prior assumptions. Team had been stuck >1 year; GPT solved it in one week using a "half-collinear regime" limit revealing the underlying structure.
- **Graviton extension**: 110 pages of novel quantum gravity calculations extending the gluon work to gravitons. The model adapted techniques specifically for gravitons' unique mathematical properties, not merely transposing the gluon approach. Lupsasca's prompt was apparently as simple as: *"Perform the same research... but instead for gravitons."*
- **Workflow pattern**: warmup priming problems → core problem → extension prompts → 3-week human verification of all 110 pages before publishing.
- **Reproduction speed**: "When GPT5 came out, it was able to reproduce one of my best papers... in 30 minutes."
- **Frontier capability claim**: "At the science frontier, the capabilities were really taking off."
- **Anchoring quote**: "ChatGPT solved this problem... before Prof. Storminger's plane even landed." (References Andrew Strominger, Lupsasca's Harvard advisor.)
- **Technique import**: "ChatGPT imported new techniques that were necessary... and used them flawlessly."
- **Distinction from AlphaFold-style**: AlphaFold applies deep learning to a well-defined existing problem (protein folding). Vibe physics is qualitatively different — novel frontier theory derived in dialogue.
- **Caveats**: Verification still requires expert humans (3 weeks for 110 pages). Reproducibility by independent groups is the next test. The article does not claim this generalizes; it documents one expert practitioner's experience at one frontier problem.
