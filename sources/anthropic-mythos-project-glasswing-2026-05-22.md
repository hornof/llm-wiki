## Summary

Digg-surfaced report (via 2026-05-22 Daily Brief "Today on Digg, not in this brief" section) that **Anthropic has operated Mythos Preview and Mythos-class models internally for three months as part of "Project Glasswing"** amid *"insufficient safeguards for public release"*. Plans named: *"work with US and allied governments after safeguards improve."* First wiki capture of **Project Glasswing** as a named Anthropic internal program. Materially updates the [[claude-mythos]] thread, which previously had only the [[antonleicht-frontier-ai-access-cut-off-2026-05-13|selective-deployment-tier framing]] without a concrete internal-program name or duration. Primary not deeply fetched; treat as practitioner-content-secondary pending direct Anthropic-side disclosure.

## Key Claims / Takeaways

### What's surfaced

- **Project Glasswing**: named internal Anthropic program governing Mythos / Mythos-class model use.
- **Three months** of internal operation prior to public release.
- *"Insufficient safeguards for public release"* — explicit framing that the internal-use posture is gated on safeguard maturity.
- **Plans include work with US and allied governments after safeguards improve** — confirms the [[claude-mythos|US-allied selective-deployment]] thesis as forward-looking strategy, not just past-deployment pattern.

### What's notably absent

- **No specific Mythos-class capabilities** disclosed in the brief synopsis.
- **No safeguard-specifics** — what *would* constitute sufficient safeguards is not named.
- **No US-government-customer specifics** — which agencies, what work scope, what budget.
- **No allied-government list** — UK? Israel? Japan? Five Eyes? Brief doesn't say.
- **No "Mythos-class" definition** — does this mean Mythos Preview successors, or a broader class of cybersecurity-tier models including selective-deployment kin?
- **No direct Anthropic confirmation** captured — Digg framing is secondary; the underlying primary source is not named in the brief.

### Why "Glasswing" matters as a name

- *"Glasswing"* — the glasswing butterfly is known for its transparent wings. The naming choice signals **transparency-as-positioning**: Anthropic frames the internal-use posture as visible-from-the-outside even when the models themselves are not publicly deployed. Worth contrasting with [[antonleicht-frontier-ai-access-cut-off-2026-05-13|Leicht's "Cut Off" framing]] where selective-deployment is structurally opaque to non-cleared parties.

## Why It Matters

- **Materially updates [[claude-mythos]] thread.** Prior coverage was [[antonleicht-frontier-ai-access-cut-off-2026-05-13|policy-analyst framing]] of Mythos as a selective-deployment-tier model alongside [[openai|OpenAI Daybreak]]. This is the **first wiki-captured Anthropic-internal-program disclosure** about Mythos with a concrete duration (3 months) and forward-deployment plan (US + allied governments).
- **Three-month internal-use duration is structurally significant.** Mythos has been wiki-tracked since [[willison-firefox-claude-mythos-2026-05|the May 6 Mozilla Firefox security-fix surge]] (Anthropic-stated 423 fixes in April under Mythos preview access). If Anthropic operated Mythos internally for 3 months *prior* to the Mozilla deployment, the internal-use period predates the wiki's first capture of Mythos by months. **The selective-deployment-tier was operational longer than the public timeline suggested.**
- **US + allied government deployment plan is the load-bearing forward signal.** Pairs with [[antonleicht-frontier-ai-access-cut-off-2026-05-13|Leicht's GAIN Act / NSA-predeployment-review / KYC machinery]] framing — the policy infrastructure Leicht names is the substrate enabling exactly the deployment plan Anthropic is signaling here. **The Leicht thesis is now operationally validated** by the Anthropic-side disclosure.
- **Project Glasswing as a named program** gives the wiki a citable handle for the internal-Anthropic-Mythos thread that didn't exist before. Worth tracking for any follow-up disclosure (Anthropic-direct, Forbes / WSJ, congressional testimony, etc.).

## Caveats

- **Digg framing is secondary.** The underlying primary source — whoever Digg is summarizing — is not named in the brief. Treat the specifics (3-month duration, "Glasswing" name, US-allied plan) as practitioner-content-secondary pending direct Anthropic disclosure or a Forbes/WSJ-tier secondary.
- **"Insufficient safeguards for public release" is Anthropic's framing if accurate** — but the brief doesn't disambiguate between Anthropic-direct quotation and Digg paraphrase. The framing is doing work: it positions the internal-use as cautious rather than competitive, even though the operational effect (selective deployment to favored partners) is the same regardless of motivation.
