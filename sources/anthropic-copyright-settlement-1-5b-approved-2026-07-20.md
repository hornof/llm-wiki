## Summary

A court approved **[[anthropic|Anthropic]]'s $1.5B class-action copyright settlement** (2026-07-20) with a group of **authors and book publishers**. The critical nuance: Judge Alsup had ruled that **training an AI model on copyrighted text is fair use** (siding with Anthropic on the core question) — the violation was **how Anthropic *acquired* the books**: it downloaded millions from **pirate sites** (Library Genesis, Pirate Library Mirror). So the $1.5B settles the *piracy/acquisition*, not the training-is-fair-use question — and because it settled, it **sets no binding appellate precedent**. *(Primary fetched.)*

## Key Claims / Takeaways

- **Amount / structure**: **$1.5B**, paid as **$3,000 per work across an estimated 500,000 works**, shared among the rights-holding authors and publishers.
- **The core ruling (Judge Alsup)**: *"training an AI model on copyrighted text counts as fair use"* — Anthropic **won** the fair-use question. Books it **purchased and scanned = fine**; books **downloaded from pirate sites (Library Genesis, Pirate Library Mirror) = the violation** (*"illegally downloaded and stored millions of copyrighted books"*).
- **What it resolves / doesn't**: settles *this specific case*. Does **not** resolve the broader fair-use-for-AI-training question as precedent — *"the case will never reach an appeals court to become binding precedent,"* so other judges remain free to rule differently.
- **Approval**: Judge **Araceli Martinez-Olguin** signed off (~2026-07-20); Judge William Alsup had granted preliminary approval "last year."
- **Broader landscape**: a *"string of copyright lawsuits"* remains against **Google, Meta, Midjourney, and OpenAI** — this settlement is Anthropic-specific, not industry-wide.

## Why it matters

- **Largest AI-copyright payout captured** — and a clean split of the legal question: **training = fair use, piracy of the corpus = liability.** The lesson labs take is about *data-acquisition provenance*, not whether training itself is legal.
- **Precedent gap persists**: a landmark dollar figure that paradoxically leaves the core legal framework unresolved (settled before appeal). Feeds the [[reverse-information-paradox|data-ownership]] / [[frontier-ai-governance|governance]] threads on how training-data rights get adjudicated.
