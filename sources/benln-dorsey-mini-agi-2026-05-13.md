benln X post 2026-05-13 surfacing a Jack Dorsey image-card with the framing *"every company can now be a mini-AGI"* (image content not captured verbatim — `pbs.twimg.com/media/HIM3IKkXcAAHt8f` is the image-only payload). ## Key Claims / Takeaways

### Dorsey's framing (image card, captured via thread)

- **"Every company can now be a mini-AGI"** is the headline framing — companies as composable intelligence rather than as workflow-orchestration containers.
- One commenter explicitly references the "roadmap becomes emergent" line from the image as the most actionable claim — i.e., explicit product-roadmap planning becomes redundant when iteration density inside an agent-mediated org crosses a threshold.

### Six practitioner reactions (the substantive payload)

1. **Pierangelo Raiola (@PierangeloRaio2, May 14)** — *"The company is not adding AI as a feature. The company becomes the model. The architecture of the org becomes the architecture of the inference. The CEO's job moves from setting strategy to designing the substrate strategy runs on."* Cleanest restatement of Dorsey's claim with operational language.
2. **Rehoboth Adam (@reowrites00, May 13)** — *"The scariest line in here: the CEO's new job is to architect the company as intelligence. Most CEOs can't even explain how their current systems work."* The CEO-competency gap framing — flags the operational reality that the move-to-intelligence-architecture imposes a literacy requirement most executives don't currently meet.
3. **Kirill Goldin (@KirillGoldinBiz, May 13)** — *"The hardest part is to change a hierarchical structure ... humans (and not just humans) organize themselves in hierarchies since the dawn of time. Do you think we'll be able to break this evolutionary"* — anthropological constraint framing; intelligence-architecture conflicts with hierarchical primate organization.
4. **FundamentalAnalysis (@FundyAnalysis, May 13)** — *"The org chart flattens before the AI is actually reliable enough to replace the layers you removed. Most companies are going to find out the hard way that judgment, taste, and creativity aren't durable human skills & they're the first things that atrophy when you gut the middle."* — single sharpest counterpoint in the thread; argues middle-management capabilities are the first to atrophy, not the most durable.
5. **Kamraj Shahapure (@KamrajShahapure, May 13)** — *"The best point here is 'Roadmap becomes emergent'"* — emergence framing for product strategy.
6. **Dan B (@DanBerger_, May 13)** — *"This feels like all theory and no reality. In theory everything should be efficient and great. In practice there is this thing called reality…"* — skeptic baseline.

- **Continues the [[ai-native-organizations]] conversation** with the cleanest "company-becomes-the-model" framing the wiki has captured. Raiola's "architecture of the org becomes the architecture of the inference" is a useful citation for the substrate / org-design boundary.

Useful counterweight to vendor-side narratives that the limiting factor is the AI.
