## Summary
April 2026 X post by @DataChaz noting that Google open-sourced DESIGN.md, a portable design-rules specification format for AI agents, via their Stitch tool. Framed competitively: Google responded to Anthropic's Claude Design rate limits by open-sourcing a vendor-neutral alternative. One sarcastic commenter dismissed it as "a glorified markdown doc with a linter."

## Key Claims / Takeaways
- **DESIGN.md**: a draft spec format (from Google Labs / Stitch) that lets you define and export design rules across projects and tools — agents read DESIGN.md to understand design intent rather than guessing
- **Stitch by Google** (@stitchbygoogle): the tool that originated DESIGN.md; being open-sourced so it works across any tool or platform, not just Google's stack
- **New capabilities added** at time of open-sourcing: export/import design rules from project to project
- **Competitive framing**: positioned as a direct counter to Anthropic's Claude Design and its rate limits; signals Google targeting AI-assisted design workflows
- **Community skepticism**: one commenter called it "a glorified markdown doc with a linter" — suggests the concept may not be technically novel, just well-positioned
