## Summary

Anthropic announced the formation of a new, as-yet-unnamed AI services company co-founded with Blackstone, Hellman & Friedman, and Goldman Sachs, and backed by General Atlantic, Leonard Green, Apollo Global Management, GIC, and Sequoia Capital. The new firm is purpose-built to deliver custom Claude implementations for mid-sized companies — community banks, regional health systems, mid-sized manufacturers — that lack the in-house resources to build frontier AI deployments. Applied AI engineers from Anthropic will embed alongside the firm's engineering team. The new company will also become a member of the Claude Partner Network.

## Key Claims / Takeaways

- **New joint venture** formed by Anthropic + Blackstone + Hellman & Friedman + Goldman Sachs; backed by General Atlantic, Leonard Green, Apollo Global Management, GIC, and Sequoia Capital. No name announced as of 2026-05-03.
- **Target market**: mid-sized companies (community banks, regional health systems, mid-sized manufacturers) — explicitly distinct from the large enterprises served by existing Claude Partner Network systems integrators (Accenture, Deloitte, PwC).
- **Delivery model**: small embedded team → understand where Claude has biggest impact → build Claude-powered systems tailored to each org's operations, with Anthropic Applied AI engineers alongside the firm's own engineers.
- **Claude Partner Network membership**: the new firm will be a member of the Claude Partner Network, extending delivery capacity beyond the existing SI ecosystem.
- **Rationale from Anthropic**: "Enterprise demand for Claude is significantly outpacing any single delivery model." — Krishna Rao, CFO, Anthropic.
- **Why mid-market**: existing systems integrators lead complex transformation programs for the world's largest enterprises; this new firm extends coverage to companies that "lack the in-house resources to build and run frontier deployments."
- **Capital structure**: founding partners are PE/finance firms (Blackstone, H&F, Goldman), not AI labs; the firm is capitalizing delivery capacity, not R&D. Anthropic contributes Applied AI engineering talent and Claude access.
- **Example engagement**: healthcare services group — clinicians spend hours on documentation, coding, prior auths, compliance; firm's engineers work with clinicians and IT to build workflow-native tools; clinicians reclaim time for patient care.
- **Sequoia Capital** is among the backers — Sequoia is already tracked as an Anthropic investor and Claude Partner Network-adjacent (hosts AI Ascent); this continues that relationship into delivery infrastructure.
