## Summary
Bloomberg Weekend Interview with Fei-Fei Li (November 2025), conducted in London where she received the 2025 Queen Elizabeth Prize for Engineering alongside Geoffrey Hinton and Jensen Huang. Wide-ranging conversation covering her background, ImageNet, spatial intelligence, AGI risk, jobs, and AI governance. The clearest single source for her position on the path to general intelligence and how it relates to current AI.

## Key Claims / Takeaways

### On Spatial Intelligence
- **Complementary to language, not a replacement**: "I believe spatial intelligence is as critical [as] — and complementary to — language intelligence." Positions spatial as an *additive* layer, not a rejection of LLMs (contrasts with LeCun's harder rejection)
- **Action-perception loop**: "We see, because we move. We move, therefore we need to see better." Physical grounding for why spatial intelligence is fundamental — not just useful
- **Spatial intelligence definition**: "the ability for AI to understand, perceive, reason and interact [with the world]... all this intricacy is centered around this capability of spatial intelligence"
- **Marble**: "a frontier model... generates a 3D world at a simple prompt" — for design, gaming, robotics simulation, AR/VR education

### On AGI and AI Risk
- **Disagrees with Hinton on extinction**: "If the human race became really in trouble, that would be the result — in my opinion — of *humans* doing wrong things, not *machines* doing wrong things"
- **AGI not imminent**: "We don't have such a machine yet. We still have a distance, a journey to take, to that day."
- **Governance framing**: "our public discourse on AI needs to move on from the question of, What do we do when [our] machine overlord is here?" — wants science-based, pragmatic approach over sci-fi framing
- **Self-description**: "I'm not a tech utopian, nor am I a dystopian — I'm actually the boring middle"

### On AI and Society
- **Jobs**: "A technology as impactful and profound as AI *will* have a profound impact on jobs" — acknowledges disruption without catastrophizing
- **Democratization**: "I would personally like to see this technology being much more democratized"
- **Human agency**: "In the age of AI, the agency should be within humans. The agency is not the machines', it's ours."
- **To parents**: Don't be lazy with AI; "If you want to understand math, maybe large language models can give you the answer, but that is not the way to learn"

### Background / Context
- Received 2025 Queen Elizabeth Prize for Engineering alongside Geoffrey Hinton and Jensen Huang
- Ran her parents' dry cleaning business from age 18 through mid-grad school
- Audacious question shifted from physics to: "What is intelligence? How do we build intelligent machines?"
- ImageNet: 22,000 classes, 15 million annotated images — enabled Hinton's neural network breakthrough at ILSVRC
