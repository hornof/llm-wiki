## Summary

April 2026 X post summarizing Jack Dorsey's framing of the central AI strategy choice: treat AI as a copilot bolted onto existing structure, or rebuild the entire company with AI as the core. Dorsey argues the copilot path leads to commoditization and eventual obsolescence. Block has been building around this thesis since early 2024, including developing Goose, an agent coding harness.

## Key Claims / Takeaways

- **Copilot = commoditization**: "If it doesn't make sense for your business to do that and you end up being or looking very similar or rhyming too closely with the frontier labs, then I think it's going to be very, very challenging to differentiate and survive."
- **Rebuild vs. augment**: Most of the industry is "thinking about AI as like a co-pilot, as something that is augmented onto" existing structures — Dorsey's view is this is the losing strategy
- **Since early 2024**: Block has been executing this thesis actively, not just theorizing; "when these tools really came to bear"
- **Goose**: Block's internal agent coding harness, built as part of the AI-native rebuild — not just a developer tool but a signal of organizational philosophy
- **Short-term vs. structural**: Speeding up old workflows with AI is a gain every competitor will match; real differentiation requires rebuilding the company itself around intelligence

## Comment Sentiment

Mixed. Pushback: AI-native startups that went API-first just became dependent on OpenAI/Anthropic (vendor risk). Skeptics: rebuilding a running company is extremely hard; incumbents can't do it. Support: human effort shifts from "correcting the system's homework" to "deciding what to do next."
