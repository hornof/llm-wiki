## Key Claims / Takeaways

### Kearns's core thesis

- *"Most AI work being sold today won't survive 2027."*
- *"The value of actually doing development is trending towards zero. It's like very very stark and very very clear that it's happening."*
- *"AI is not its own bucket. Every single vertical will just have AI seeping into it."*
- *"When I get a 67-year-old lawyer coming to me with a vibe-coded app that he's built that's like decent, that's obviously signal that the talent and the skill set of doing development [is collapsing as a defensible position]."*

### Lifestyle-business vs enterprise-value framing (the load-bearing strategic choice)

- *"As a pure AI readiness consultant, $2 million a year — your profit margin, you're going to be able to sell that business for probably $2 million. But if you're making $6 million a year, then it's like 'Oh, now I can sell this business for like 5×, I can sell it for $30 million.'"*
- The structural choice: **lifestyle business** (lifestyle income, no real enterprise value) vs **enterprise-value business** (scaled organization, sellable for 5× revenue).
- Kearns chose the latter. Implication: most AI agency operators are unconsciously building lifestyle businesses; the meta-question of building toward enterprise-value gets unnamed.

### The inflection point (Kearns's call on why now)

- *"Opus 4.6 happened and Open Claw happened. All of the updates that Anthropic has been pushing within the Claude ecosystem are all just wrappers around the model being better."*
- *"The early majority of companies are starting to come online to AI with Claude, with Cloud Code, the massive wave of updates and model improvements that have happened over the last four months."*
- **Pairs with [[nateherk-caio-opportunity-2026-05-17|IBM CAIO data: 76% adoption up from 26% in 2024]]** — both surface the **early-majority-coming-online** as the now-or-never strategic window.

### What survives (per Kearns)

- *"Across the board org charts are shifting and changing, they're just consolidating."*
- AI agencies that survive are positioned as **outcomes-and-architecture consultants**, not **execution consultants**.
- Custom AI Studio's posture: *"architecting, designing, building and deploying custom AI systems for clients of all sizes from publicly traded enterprises to solo founders... typically in the mid-market space."*
- Evolution arc: *"$2,500 NAD in automations in the early days"* → *"$4-500K projects over a course of a year with ongoing managed service contracts."*

### Self-described preconditions

- *"A lot of people think they want this opportunity to start an agency, but then they actually don't. They don't want the sales. Maybe they just want to be more of the builder."*
- *"Honestly, most people probably shouldn't do their own thing — it's probably not ideal because it's competitive and most people fail."*
- The path requires: sales-comfort + scale-ambition + enterprise-value-orientation. Without all three, lifestyle business is the realistic ceiling.
