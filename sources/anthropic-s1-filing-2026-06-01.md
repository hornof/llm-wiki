## Summary

[[anthropic|Anthropic, PBC]] confidentially submits a **draft S-1 registration statement to the U.S. SEC** for a proposed initial public offering of common stock (2026-06-01). Announcement published under Rule 135 of the Securities Act of 1933. **First wiki-captured Anthropic IPO filing event**, **4 days after the [[anthropic-series-h-65b-965b-2026-05-28|Series H $65B raise at $965B valuation]]** + 1 day after the [[anthropic-how-we-contain-claude-2026-05-31|containment playbook]] + [[anthropic-knowledge-work-plugins-2026-05-31|plugins repo publication]]. **Second wiki-captured frontier-lab IPO filing in 12 days** — joins [[openai-ipo-filing-2026-05-20|OpenAI's WSJ-reported IPO filing]] (May 20). **Both frontier labs are now en route to public markets simultaneously.**

## Key Claims / Takeaways

### What was filed

- **Anthropic, PBC** (the public-benefit corporation entity) — same legal entity that runs Claude and the Anthropic API
- **Draft S-1 registration statement** — the standard IPO registration document required by the SEC
- **Confidential submission** — Rule 135 allows the company to file privately while the SEC reviews; public S-1 (with financials) comes later
- **Number of shares + price not yet set** — standard early-stage IPO posture; will be determined closer to listing
- **Conditional on market conditions** — Anthropic explicitly preserves optionality to not proceed

### Pairs with [[openai-ipo-filing-2026-05-20|OpenAI's IPO filing]]

**Both frontier labs are simultaneously en route to public markets** for the first time. Strategic implications:

- **Public-market reference price** for AI-infrastructure-displacing-application-SaaS valuation gets set by both labs' first-trading-day pricing
- **Capital-allocation regime change**: post-IPO, both labs are accountable quarterly. Anthropic's $14B-projected-2026-losses (per [[chamath-openai-consulting-fox-in-henhouse-2026-05-17|Chamath framing]]) become structurally harder to sustain under public-market scrutiny
- **Comparison-pricing market**: investors will compare Anthropic ($965B private) and OpenAI (~$500B last private per [[dailybrief-supplemental-2026-05-31|Qazinform tertiary]] though disputed) directly on the same metrics — revenue, growth rate, gross margin, capex, training-data spend ([[data-as-moat-frontier-2026-05-30|$10-15B/yr]])
- **Talent-flow regime change**: post-IPO equity becomes liquid; the [[lennysan-dream-companies-survey-2026-05-27|Anthropic talent-magnet trajectory]] gets a *liquid-equity exit-vehicle* dimension that pre-IPO compensation packages lack

**Pattern-watch**: which lab actually lists first? Anthropic's confidential filing today means SEC review starts now (typical: 2-4 months); OpenAI's May 20 WSJ-reported filing presumably started its review ~12 days earlier. **First-mover advantage on public-market pricing goes to whoever lists first.**
