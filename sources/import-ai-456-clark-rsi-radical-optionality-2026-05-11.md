## Summary

[[jack-clark]] (Anthropic; editor of *Import AI*) issue #456, dated May 11, 2026. Three pieces:

1. A **recursive self-improvement (RSI) and economic-growth** modeling result from Anton Korinek and collaborators: with full automation of software R&D plus only **5% automation elsewhere**, the model predicts an "automation shock" producing a singularity-grade growth regime in ~6 years; **13% economy-wide automation** is sufficient to push the economy into the "explosive regime."
2. A policy frame Clark calls **"radical optionality for AI regulation"** — preserving democratic governments' ability to make good decisions about how to govern transformative AI systems *as circumstances evolve*, rather than locking in current-state regulation.
3. A research note on **neural computers** (Meta / KAIST work led by Jürgen Schmidhuber and collaborators) — a prototype of CLI and GUI variants where the OS is a generative-video model. Vision: "a fully learned computer whose compute, memory, and interfaces are unified in a single learned runtime substrate rather than engineered as separate modules."

## Key Claims / Takeaways

- **RSI modeling result (Korinek et al.)**:
  - "13% automation across all sectors is sufficient to push the economy into the explosive regime."
  - Hardware R&D is the dominant lever in the model.
  - An "automation shock" involving full automation of software R&D and just 5% automation across the rest of the economy causes the singularity to arrive in roughly six years per the simulation.
  - Anton Korinek now works with Clark at Anthropic — establishes an Anthropic-house result, not just a citation.
- **Radical optionality (Clark's policy frame)**:
  - "Preserving democratic governments' ability to make good decisions about how to govern transformative AI systems as circumstances evolve."
  - Endorses lightweight instruments: transparency / reporting mandates, whistleblower protections, inter-governmental info-sharing, flexible conditional regulations, assessment capacity-building, and talent investment at AISI / CAISI-style agencies.
  - "The cost of implementing these policies is modest, relative to the potential benefits."
  - Explicitly *not* a call for pre-emptive heavy regulation — rejects the heavy/light binary in favor of an option-preserving posture.
- **Neural computers**:
  - Meta / KAIST team led by Jürgen Schmidhuber prototype CLI and GUI variants using generative-video models.
  - Clark: software lives "in the weights of a big neural net."
  - Mature versions need "10T-1000T" scale machines (i.e., 10-1000 trillion parameters or compute units — context-dependent; verify in source).
  - Companion datapoint: Google's distributed training already functions across four U.S. regions — substrate independence is becoming infrastructural reality.
