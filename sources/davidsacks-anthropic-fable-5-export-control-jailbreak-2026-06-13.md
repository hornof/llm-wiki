## Key Claims / Takeaways

### The Sacks 8-point thread (verbatim structural summary)

1. **Anthropic released Mythos commercially as Fable** earlier this week (Jun 9 per [[claudeai-fable-5-mythos-5-official-launch-2026-06-09|official launch]])
2. **Fable = Mythos + guardrails** — if guardrails fail, Mythos cyber-capabilities exposed. *"Keep in mind that Anthropic itself widely promoted the idea that Mythos was a cyberweapon and needed to be regulated as such. They asked for government regulation of Mythos and championed the guardrails on Fable. If there is a vulnerability — big or small — it is Anthropic's responsibility to patch."*
3. **A highly credible trusted partner** of both Anthropic + USG who was testing Fable came forward with a **jailbreak of the guardrails**. The Admin asked Dario to fix the jailbreak or de-deploy the model. **Dario refused.**
4. **Anthropic blog-post defense**: said the jailbreak isn't serious. *"That is not what the trusted partner and the USG believe; nor is that kind of minimizing language consistent with Anthropic's brand as the AI safety company. It's difficult to fathom how they could claim a jailbreak allowing operability of a cyber weapon could be defined as not 'serious.'"*
5. **Anthropic prioritized continued consumer-model offering over safety.** *"In the past, Anthropic has always said that safety must be top priority and taken super seriously. In this case, Anthropic prioritized the continued offering of the consumer model over safety."*
6. **Admin issued export control** — **reluctantly**. *"It's been very surprised that Anthropic hasn't wanted to cooperate with a reasonable safety request (ie fixing the jailbreak issue). Anthropic's reaction is very much at odds with their branding and ethos as a safe AI research community."*
7. **Path forward**: Admin's hope is that **Anthropic remediates → export control lifted → Fable returns to general release**. *"The Admin wants all of this to happen as soon as possible. It is frankly bewildered that Anthropic hasn't wanted to comply with safety requests that it previously said were its highest priority."*
8. **Disclaimer**: *"Those trying to misdirect and tie this action to the prior DoW/Anthropic issues are wrong. The Admin values Anthropic's technical capabilities and feels that this issue, while serious, should be easily resolved. The ball is in Anthropic's court."*
