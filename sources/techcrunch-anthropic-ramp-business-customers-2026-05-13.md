## Summary

TechCrunch (May 13, 2026) reports Ramp's AI Index — drawn from **50,000+ companies** on Ramp's expense platform — now shows **34.4% of participating businesses pay for Anthropic** vs. **32.3% for OpenAI**, a 2.1-percentage-point lead and the **first time Anthropic has held the top position** on this index. Twelve months prior (May 2025), Anthropic was at ~9%; OpenAI declined ~1% over the same period. Cross-confirmed indirectly by OpenRouter data, where OpenAI "last ranked above Anthropic in December 2025."

## Key Claims / Takeaways

- **Ramp AI Index May 2026**: Anthropic **34.4%** vs. OpenAI **32.3%** (2.1pp lead). Sample: 50,000+ Ramp-customer companies.
- **First-time lead**: TechCrunch frames this as "the first time Anthropic has held the top position" on the Ramp index.
- **12-month trajectory**: Anthropic 9% → 35.4% in May 2026 (≈26pp swing in one year). OpenAI declined ~1% over the same period.
- **OpenRouter cross-confirm**: separate developer-traffic dataset places OpenAI's last lead over Anthropic in **December 2025** — the index-vs-OpenRouter agreement makes this less likely to be a Ramp-sample artifact.
- **Methodology caveat (in-article)**: "Because the index only represents companies that use Ramp, it's not a perfect proxy for the marketplace at large." Ramp's customer base skews toward US-headquartered finance/tech/professional-services companies that adopt new SaaS quickly.
- **Ramp economist Ara Kharazian quote**: "Anthropic has already been in the lead amongst the high adoption groups like finance, tech, professional services… Anthropic's strategy was — start with a very technical customer base, focus on their needs, really succeed in execution and then start broadening out."
- No quotes from Anthropic or OpenAI representatives in the article.
